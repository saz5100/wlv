# Worcester vs WLV vs AQA — Key Differences

This page compares the University of Worcester English Equivalency test with the University of Wolverhampton (WLV) Equivalency test and the standard AQA Paper 1 (8700/1) examination.

## Assessment Structure

| Feature | Worcester | WLV | AQA Paper 1 |
|---------|-----------|-----|-------------|
| Total marks | 50 | 50 | 80 |
| Reading marks | 25 | 25 | 40 |
| Writing marks | 25 | 25 | 40 |
| Time | 2 hours | 2 hours | 1 hour 45 minutes |
| Delivery | Online (Collaborate) | On-campus | On-campus |

## Reading Section

| Feature | Worcester | WLV | AQA Paper 1 |
|---------|-----------|-----|-------------|
| Source type | Single unseen extract | Single unseen extract | Single unseen extract |
| Q1 format | Multiple-choice retrieval (4 marks) | Multiple-choice retrieval (4 marks) | Multiple-choice retrieval (4 marks) |
| Q2 format | Inference from simile (2 marks) | Language analysis (8 marks) | Language analysis (8 marks) |
| Q3 format | Tone identification (2 marks) | Structural analysis (8 marks) | Structural analysis (8 marks) |
| Q4 format | Character interpretation (3 marks) | Not tested | Evaluation (20 marks) |
| Q5 format | Summarise relationship (5 marks) | Not tested | Not tested |
| Q6 format | Summarise views (5 marks) | Not tested | Not tested |
| Question delivery | Oral (Q3–6) | Written | Written |
| Structural analysis | Not tested | Tested (8 marks) | Tested (8 marks) |
| Language analysis | Not tested | Tested (8 marks) | Tested (8 marks) |

## Writing Section

| Feature | Worcester | WLV | AQA Paper 1 |
|---------|-----------|-----|-------------|
| Number of prompts | 3 (choose 1) | 1 (fixed) | 2 (choose 1) |
| Prompt themes | Career, story, persuasion | Descriptive/narrative | Descriptive/narrative |
| Submission method | Handwritten + scan | Handwritten | Handwritten |
| SPaG weighting | Not specified | 50% of writing marks | 40% of writing marks |

## Key Implications for Candidates

- **Worcester candidates** must prepare for oral questioning, summarisation, tone identification, and career-focused writing.
- **WLV candidates** should focus on language analysis, structural analysis, and descriptive/narrative writing.
- **AQA candidates** need to prepare for evaluation (Q4) and have writing choice.
- Candidates taking the Worcester test should **not** spend revision time on structural analysis or language analysis frameworks, as these are not tested.
