# Research Methods

## Introduction to Research Methods

### Research Design
- **Research paradigms**: Understanding positivist, interpretivist, and critical approaches
- **Quantitative research**: Experimental designs, surveys, and statistical analysis
- **Qualitative research**: Case studies, ethnography, phenomenology, and grounded theory
- **Mixed methods**: Combining quantitative and qualitative approaches
- **Research questions**: Formulating clear, focused, and researchable questions

### Data Collection
- **Surveys and questionnaires**: Designing effective questions and sampling strategies
- **Interviews**: Structured, semi-structured, and unstructured interview techniques
- **Observations**: Participant and non-participant observation methods
- **Document analysis**: Using existing texts, records, and archival materials
- **Experiments**: Laboratory and field experiments, control groups, and variables

### Data Analysis
- **Quantitative analysis**: Descriptive and inferential statistics, data visualisation
- **Qualitative analysis**: Thematic analysis, content analysis, and discourse analysis
- **Coding and categorisation**: Developing and applying coding frameworks
- **Interpretation**: Drawing meaning from data and relating findings to theory
- **Software tools**: Using SPSS, NVivo, R, or other analysis software

### Ethics
- **Ethical principles**: Respect for persons, beneficence, justice, and integrity
- **Informed consent**: Ensuring participants understand the research and their rights
- **Confidentiality and anonymity**: Protecting participant identity and data
- **Ethical approval**: Navigating institutional ethics review processes
- **Research integrity**: Avoiding fabrication, falsification, and plagiarism in research
