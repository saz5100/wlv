# Q1 Retrieval Strategy

## What Q1 Tests

Q1 tests your ability to identify and retrieve **explicit information** from a text. You are given a short, clearly indicated section of the source and asked to find **4 specific facts**. Worth **4 marks** (1 mark per correct fact).

## The Method — 5 Steps

| Step | Time | What to do |
|------|:----:|-----------|
| 1. Read the question | 30s | Identify what the question asks you to find |
| 2. Locate the lines | 30s | Go directly to the specified line range |
| 3. Skim for facts | 1 min | Look for concrete details, not opinions |
| 4. Select 4 facts | 1 min | Choose the clearest, most explicit pieces of information |
| 5. Write them down | 1 min | Use short phrases or bullet points |

**Total: ~4 minutes**

## What Counts as a Fact

A fact is something **explicitly stated** in the text:
- What a character did ("The boy ran towards the house")
- What something looked like ("The door was painted red")
- Where something happened ("They were in a narrow alley")
- When something occurred ("It was early morning")
- What someone said ("She whispered, 'Help me'")

## What Does NOT Count

| Wrong approach | Why |
|---------------|-----|
| Inference ("He was scared because...") | Not explicit — you're guessing |
| Opinion ("The description was vivid") | Your opinion, not in the text |
| Summary ("The opening describes a storm") | Too vague — needs specific detail |
| Quoting too much | Slows you down — use short phrases |

## 2026 Update — Multiple Choice Format

From 2026, Q1 changes to a **multiple-choice** format: you shade circles to select the 4 correct statements. This makes it faster but requires careful reading to avoid trick answers.

## Model Answer (Traditional Format)

**Question:** List four things about the Master from lines 1-5.

1. The Master was slightly peculiar.
2. He had travelled abroad.
3. He owned a pet.
4. He lived alone.

Each gets 1 mark. No extra explanation needed.

## WLV-Specific Note

The WLV test uses **single-source extraction** — all reading questions (Q1-Q3) use the same source extract. You only need to retrieve facts from one text, not compare across texts.

## Common Q1 Mistakes

* Writing 3 instead of 4 facts — always count your points
* Inferring rather than retrieving — only write what's explicitly there
* Writing full sentences — short phrases are faster and clearer
* Copying huge chunks of text — wastes time
