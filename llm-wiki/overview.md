# Wiki Overview

This wiki is a comprehensive revision resource for the University of Wolverhampton (WLV) English Equivalency test, systematically mapping AQA Paper 1 (8700/1) skills to the WLV assessment specification. It covers all testable areas: explicit retrieval, language analysis, structural analysis, and creative writing, with a strong emphasis on SPaG (spelling, punctuation, and grammar).

## Reading Skills

The reading component focuses on analysing a single unseen 19th, 20th, or 21st-century literary or non-fiction prose extract. Students must demonstrate the ability to retrieve explicit information (Q1), analyse the writer's use of language (Q2), and examine structural features such as focus shifts and narrative architecture (Q3). The wiki provides frameworks, annotated examples, and practice materials for each skill. The June 2022 paper, featuring an extract from *The Pearl* by John Steinbeck, and the June 2023 paper, featuring an extract from *Life of Pi* by Yann Martel, offer excellent practice for all three reading skills. The official AQA mark schemes for June 2022 and June 2023 provide detailed level descriptors and indicative content for all reading questions, including the four-level hierarchy used for assessment. The June 2023 question paper has been ingested, providing the exact wording for all five questions, including the Q4 evaluation statement ('funny rather than frightening') and the Q5 wildlife-themed writing prompts. The *City of the Beasts* mock paper provides additional practice with a text focused on illness and family dynamics, expanding the thematic range of available practice materials.

The AQA 8700/1 Sample Mark Scheme (June 2026 first exam) introduces several important updates to the assessment framework. Question 1 has changed from short-answer written responses to a multiple-choice format (shade the circle, 4 marks). The sample mark scheme establishes a consistent four-level hierarchy across all questions with progression keywords, and provides detailed indicative standards for each level. A new cap rule limits off-task Q5 responses to a maximum of 12 marks (top of Level 2) for AO5. The document also formalises a gender-inclusive marking policy, confirming that the use of 'they/them' as a singular pronoun and pronouns beyond 'she/her' or 'he/him' will be credited in exam responses. The sample source text is from *Life of Pi* (hyena extract), providing additional practice material for this text.

The newly ingested **AQA June 2024 Insert** features an extract from *The Grass is Singing* (1950) by Doris Lessing, set in Southern Africa. This extract introduces a new sub-genre — domestic realism with a psychological focus — to the wiki's collection of source texts. Unlike the adventure/survival narratives of *The Pearl* and *Life of Pi*, this extract centres on environmental oppression and marital conflict, offering rich material for sensory language analysis (Q2) and structural focus shifts (Q3). New entity pages have been created for the novel's context and the protagonist Mary Turner, and new concept pages cover 'heat as obsession' and 'third-person limited narration', complementing the existing first-person limited narration page.

The **November 2024 Mark Scheme** further expands the wiki's analytical depth, using an extract from *The Glass House* by Eve Chase. It provides critical examples of "perceptive" analysis, particularly regarding the contrast between idealised dreams and bleak realities. It highlights the use of flashbacks and specific similes (e.g., "like a snapped spine") to create atmospheric tension and structural foreboding.

## Writing Skills

The writing component requires students to produce a 400-word descriptive or narrative piece (Q5), with marks awarded for content, organisation, and technical accuracy. The wiki includes guides on creative writing structure, tone, and device usage, alongside SPaG reference pages covering comma splicing, apostrophes, sentence demarcation, colons, semicolons, and dashes. The June 2022 paper offers two writing options: a description of an unusual place to stay (picture prompt) or a story about a life-saving rescue. The June 2023 paper offers two wildlife-themed options: a description of a zoo or wildlife park (picture prompt) or a story about a human meeting an animal. The June 2023 mark scheme provides the official AO5 (Content and Organisation) and AO6 (Technical Accuracy) marking criteria, including the subdivision of Level 4 into Upper and Lower bands, and detailed descriptors for sentence demarcation, punctuation range, sentence forms, Standard English, grammar, spelling, and vocabulary. The *City of the Beasts* mock paper offers two additional writing options: a description of a stormy sea (picture prompt) or a story beginning with a given sentence about a terrible day. The sample mark scheme for June 2026 confirms the AO5 and AO6 marking structures and introduces the cap rule for off-task responses.

The November 2024 mark scheme reinforces the requirement for "convincing and compelling" communication and "consistently secure" sentence demarcation for the highest marks in AO6.

## Source Materials

The wiki indexes past papers, source texts, and official mark schemes used in AQA Paper 1 examinations. Currently ingested sources include the June 2022 insert (featuring an extract from *The Pearl* by John Steinbeck), the June 2022 question paper, and its corresponding mark scheme, the June 2023 insert (featuring an extract from *Life of Pi* by Yann Martel), the June 2023 mark scheme, the June 2023 question paper, the *City of the Beasts* mock paper, the AQA 8700/1 Sample Mark Scheme (June 2026 first exam), and the June 2024 insert (featuring an extract from *The Grass is Singing* by Doris Lessing). The November 2024 mark scheme, based on *The Glass House*, has also been added. A dedicated page lists known AQA Paper 1 source authors to guide practice material selection. All sources are analysed for their relevance to the WLV equivalency test. The *City of the Beasts* source has generated dedicated entity pages for the novel's context and the protagonist Alex Cold. The *The Grass is Singing* source has generated entity pages for the novel's context and the protagonist Mary Turner.

## Key Concepts

Core concepts such as 'unseen texts', 'source types', 'first-person limited narration', 'cyclical structure', and 'sensory accumulation' are defined and cross-linked, providing students with a clear understanding of the genre, period, and analytical expectations. The ingestion of the June 2023 and June 2022 mark schemes has added a comprehensive set of assessment-related concepts, including the four-level marking hierarchy, indicative standards, and specific marking criteria for each assessment objective (AO1, AO2, AO4, AO5, AO6). Structural concepts such as 'anti-climax as a structural device' and 'structural features for analysis' have been added to support Q3 analysis. The *City of the Beasts* mock paper introduced two concepts: 'dream as a literary device' and 'illness and family as a theme'. The sample mark scheme for June 2026 added concepts including 'level-keywords', 'marking-procedure', 'gender-inclusive-marking', and 'writing-task-cap-rule'. The June 2024 insert has added two new concepts: 'heat as obsession' (environmental oppression as a literary device) and 'third-person limited narration' (complementing the existing first-person limited narration page). 

The November 2024 mark scheme has introduced the concept of "Indicative Standards" and provided detailed "Indicative Content" for critical evaluation, further clarifying how "perceptive" analysis is measured.

## WLV-Specific Content

The newly ingested **WLV English Equivalency Blueprint** provides a targeted revision map for the University of Wolverhampton's 2-hour on-campus assessment. This document confirms that the WLV test is a streamlined subset of AQA Paper 1 mechanics, removing Paper 2 synthesis, Q4 evaluation, the Spoken Language Endorsement, and writing prompt choice. The blueprint introduces several key structural differences: a single-source extract for all reading questions, a fixed thematic writing prompt with no choice, and a 50% SPaG weighting in the writing section. New concept pages have been created for 'single-source extraction', 'fixed thematic prompt', 'condensed assessment layout', and 'spag weighting'. A dedicated overview page ([[wlv-english-equivalency-overview]]) and a comparison page ([[wlv-vs-aqa-differences]]) have been added to help WLV candidates focus their revision effectively. The blueprint confirms that AQA Paper 1 past papers remain the best practice resource, but only Questions 1, 2, 3, and 5 should be attempted.

## Scope Notes

The wiki is designed specifically for the WLV English Equivalency test. Questions 1, 2, 3, and 5 from AQA Paper 1 are fully in scope. Question 4 (evaluation) is included in AQA papers but is marked as out of scope for the WLV assessment. Users should confirm this with their specific test specification. The official mark schemes for June 2022 and June 2023 have been ingested primarily for their Q1, Q2, Q3, and Q5 content, though Q4 marking criteria are also available for reference. The June 2023 question paper is useful for practising Q1–Q3 and Q5 skills, with Q4 available for optional critical reading practice. The *City of the Beasts* mock paper provides full practice for all five questions. The sample mark scheme for June 2026 is relevant for understanding the updated Q1 format (multiple-choice) and the new marking policies (gender-inclusive language, cap rule), though users should confirm whether these changes apply to the WLV equivalency test. The June 2024 insert provides additional practice for Q1–Q3 skills with a psychological fiction extract. The WLV English Equivalency Blueprint confirms that the Spoken Language Endorsement, Paper 2 synthesis, Q4 evaluation, and writing choice are all out of scope for the WLV test.
