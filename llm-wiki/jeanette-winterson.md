# Jeanette Winterson

Jeanette Winterson (born 1959) is a British author known for her distinctive literary style that blends autobiography, fiction, and philosophical reflection. Her work often explores themes of identity, family, sexuality, and the power of storytelling.

## Key Works

- *Oranges Are Not the Only Fruit* (1985) — a semi-autobiographical novel about a young girl growing up in a Pentecostal community
- *Why Be Happy When You Could Be Normal?* (2011) — a memoir exploring her childhood, adoption, and relationship with her mother
- *The Passion* (1987) — a historical novel set in Napoleonic Europe
- *Written on the Body* (1992) — a novel about love and loss

- First-person narrative voice
- Blends humour with pathos
- Uses vivid imagery and figurative language
- Employs short, punchy sentences for impact
- Often reflective and analytical about her own experiences
