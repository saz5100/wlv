# AQA Indicative Standard

The AQA mark scheme includes 'indicative standards' for each level. These are explicitly **not model answers** but rather indications of the standard of response expected at each level.

## Key Principles

- **Not a model answer:** The indicative standard shows the type and quality of response, not a perfect answer to be replicated.
- **Level-specific:** Each level has its own indicative standard showing the progression from simple to perceptive.
- **Working document:** The mark scheme is a working document that may be further developed based on students' reactions in the examination.

## Example (June 2023 Q4 Level 4)

The Level 4 indicative standard for Q4 argues that the hyena's behaviour is 'definitely more amusing' than frightening, using the circular motion as a symbol for pointless behaviour. This is approximately 150 words and demonstrates the depth and perceptiveness expected at Level 4.
