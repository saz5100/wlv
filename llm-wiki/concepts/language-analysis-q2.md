# Language Analysis (Q2)

Question 2 of AQA Paper 1 requires students to analyse how a writer uses language to convey a specific feeling, atmosphere, or character trait.

## Analytical Focus
Students are expected to examine:
- **Words and Phrases**: Specific diction and the connotations they carry.
- **Language Features**: Similes, metaphors, personification, and adjectives.
- **Sentence Forms**: How the length or structure of a sentence contributes to the mood.

## Example: *The Grass is Singing*
In the June 2024 paper, the focus was on the oppressive nature of heat. High-level responses identified:
- **Violent Imagery**: "heat waves beat up" suggests the environment is an aggressor.
- **Similes**: "lizards... like flames" emphasises the transformative and destructive power of the temperature.

## Success Criteria
A "Perceptive" (Level 4) answer does not simply list these devices but explains how they create a sense of psychological entrapment or physical exhaustion for the character.
