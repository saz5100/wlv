# Sentence Classifications

Sentences are classified by their structure—specifically the number and type of clauses they contain.

## Simple Sentences
Contains **one main clause** and no subordinate clauses.
- *Example: "The dog barked."*

## Compound Sentences
Contains **two or more main clauses** joined by a coordinating conjunction.
- **The ASBO Rule**: Coordinating conjunctions can be remembered by the acronym **ASBO** (**A**nd, **S**o, **B**ut, **O**r).
- *Example: "The dog barked, and the cat ran away."*

## Complex Sentences
Contains **one main clause** and **at least one subordinate clause**.
- *Example: "The dog barked because it saw a squirrel."*
