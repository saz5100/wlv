# Structural Analysis (Q3)

Question 3 focuses on the "architecture" of the text—how the writer organises the information to control the reader's experience.

## Key Structural Elements
- **Openings**: How the text establishes the setting or mood.
- **Shifts in Focus**: Moving from a wide shot (environment) to a close-up (character emotion).
- **Temporal Shifts**: The use of flashbacks or changes in the passage of time.
- **Narrative Movement**: Moving from outside to inside, or from one character's perspective to another.

## Example: *The Grass is Singing*
The June 2024 mark scheme highlights:
- **Exposition**: Establishing Mary's breaking point early in the text.
- **Temporal Erosion**: Phrases like "as time passed" showing the loss of a grip on reality.
- **Spatial Shift**: The movement from the oppressive outdoors to the interior of the house.

## Success Criteria
Top-tier responses analyse the *effect* of these shifts. For example, explaining how the absence of specific temporal markers suggests a character's descent into madness or disorientation.
