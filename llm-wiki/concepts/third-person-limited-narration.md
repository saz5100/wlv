# Third-Person Limited Narration

Third-person limited narration is a narrative mode in which the story is told from the perspective of a single character using third-person pronouns ('she', 'he', 'they'), but the reader only has access to that character's thoughts, feelings, and perceptions. The narrator remains outside the story but is 'limited' to the viewpoint of one character.

## Key Features

- **Pronouns:** Uses 'she', 'he', 'they' rather than 'I'.
- **Focalisation:** The narrative is filtered through one character's consciousness.
- **Restricted knowledge:** The reader knows only what the focal character knows, thinks, and feels.
- **Subjective presentation:** Events are presented through the character's subjective experience, not an objective or omniscient viewpoint.

## Example from *The Grass is Singing*

In the AQA June 2024 extract, the narration is limited to Mary Turner's perspective. The reader experiences the heat through her senses and emotions:

- 'she would watch the heat-waves beat up out of the hot stone'
- 'she could not bear the sapping, undermining waves'
- 'Mary could hear them panting softly'

The reader does not have access to Dick Turner's thoughts or feelings — only to Mary's perception of his anger.

## Analytical Application

When analysing third-person limited narration, consider:

1. **What is the reader allowed to see?** What does the focal character notice or ignore?
2. **How does the limited perspective create sympathy or distance?** The reader may feel closer to the focal character.
3. **What is withheld?** The technique can create suspense or ambiguity by restricting information.
4. **How does the narration shift?** Does the text ever move outside the character's perspective?
