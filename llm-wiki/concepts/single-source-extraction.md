# Single-Source Extraction

Single-source extraction refers to the WLV English Equivalency test's use of a single unseen prose extract for the entire reading section, in contrast to the full AQA GCSE which uses two texts for Paper 2 comparison and synthesis.

## Key Characteristics

- Only one extract is provided for all reading questions (Q1, Q2, Q3).
- The extract may be literary or non-fiction prose from the 19th, 20th, or 21st century.
- No cross-text comparison or synthesis is required.

## Implications for Revision

- Practise close reading of a single extract rather than comparing multiple texts.
- Focus on depth of analysis within one passage rather than breadth across texts.
- All reading skills (retrieval, language analysis, structural analysis) are applied to the same extract.
