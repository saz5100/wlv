# Writing Content and Organisation Levels

The marking for the content of the writing task (AO5) is divided into levels that reflect the maturity and control of the writer's craft.

## Level Breakdown

| Level | Quality | Characteristics |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Level 4** | **Compelling** | Sophisticated, imaginative, and highly controlled. Seamless integration of structural features. |
| **Level 3** | **Clear** | Effective communication with a clear sense of purpose and generally consistent tone. |
| **Level 2** | **Some** | Basic communication; some attempt at structure but may be repetitive or inconsistent. |
| **Level 1** | **Simple** | Limited communication; simple vocabulary and random or absent paragraphing. |

## Strategic Advice for WLV Students
Given the 400-word limit, students should focus on "quality over quantity." A concise, highly polished piece with ambitious vocabulary and a clear structural arc is more likely to hit Level 4 than a longer, rambling narrative.
