# Split Infinitives

A split infinitive occurs when a word (usually an adverb) is placed between the word 'to' and the verb.

## British vs American Standards
- **British English**: In formal academic and examination writing, splitting an infinitive is considered a grammatical error.
- **American English**: This is generally acceptable and common. The OCR guide notes that American influence (e.g., through media like *Star Trek*) has made split infinitives more common in spoken British English.

## Examples
- **Incorrect (Split)**: "To quickly run to the store."
- **Correct (Unsplit)**: "To run quickly to the store."

For the WLV assessment, students should avoid splitting infinitives to maintain a formal, academic tone.
