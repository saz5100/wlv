# Quotation Mechanics

Integrating quotations into academic writing requires specific formatting to maintain clarity and professional standards.

## Embedded Quotations
Short quotations are integrated directly into the sentence using inverted commas.
- **Line Breaks**: When quoting poetry or verse, a forward slash (/) is used to indicate a line break.
- **Modifications**: Square brackets [ ] are used to alter a word or phrase within a quote to make it fit the grammar of the surrounding sentence.

## Long (Block) Quotations
Quotations that are significantly long should be formatted as a block:
1. Start the quotation on a new line.
2. Indent the entire block of text.
3. Introduce the quote with a colon.
4. **Note**: Inverted commas are not required for block quotes unless the original text contains dialogue.

## Terminology: Quote vs Quotation
While "quote" is increasingly used as a noun in journalism (e.g., *The Times*), "quotation" remains the required academic standard for examinations.
- **Verb**: "I will quote the author." (Correct)
- **Noun**: "This is a beautiful quotation." (Preferred over "This is a beautiful quote.")
