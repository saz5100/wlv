# Absence as Presence

## Definition

Absence as Presence is a literary device where a character, object, or theme that is physically absent or marginal still exerts a dominant, pervasive force over the narrative. The absence is emphasised so heavily that it feels like an active presence, influencing the actions, thoughts, and emotions of other characters.

## How It Works

1. **Emotional impact:** The absence of a loved one creates a deep emotional void that both the characters and reader feel acutely.

2. **Theme reinforcement:** The device reinforces themes of loss, longing, memory, and the inability to move on.

3. **Character revelation:** How characters respond to absence reveals their personality, coping mechanisms, and deepest fears.

## Analysing in the Exam

When analysing absence as presence, consider:

- **What is absent?** A person, an object, a feeling, a memory?
- **How is the absence shown?** Through dialogue? Through what is NOT said? Through empty spaces and silences?
- **What effect does the absence have?** Does it create tension, sadness, mystery, or fear?
- **Structural position:** Does the absence appear at the beginning (creating a mystery) or end (leaving ambiguity)?
