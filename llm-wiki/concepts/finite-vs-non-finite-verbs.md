# Finite vs Non-Finite Verbs

The distinction between finite and non-finite verbs is crucial for identifying the main clause of a sentence.

## Finite Verbs
A finite verb is a "working" verb that:
- Has a specific **tense** (past, present, future).
- Agrees with its **subject** (singular or plural).
- Can function as the main verb of a clause.

## Non-Finite Verbs
Non-finite verbs do not show tense or agree with a subject. They typically appear as:
- **Infinitives**: The base form of the verb, often preceded by 'to' (e.g., *to eat*).
- **-ing forms (Participles/Gerunds)**: Verbs ending in -ing (e.g., *running*).
