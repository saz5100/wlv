# Storm as Emotional Landscape

## Definition

The storm as emotional landscape is a literary device where a physical storm (wind, rain, thunder, waves) is used to mirror a character's inner emotional state. This is a form of pathetic fallacy, where the external environment externalises and amplifies the character's internal feelings.

## Functions

1. **Mood establishment:** A storm at the opening instantly creates tension, danger, or turmoil. The reader knows something significant is happening.

2. **Character mirroring:** A character's anger might be accompanied by thunder; their grief by relentless rain; their confusion by swirling wind.

3. **Foreshadowing:** A gathering storm often foreshadows conflict or crisis to come.

4. **Contrast:** A calm after a storm can mirror a character's resolution or acceptance.

## Analysis Questions

- Is the weather literal or symbolic?
- Does the storm build gradually or arrive suddenly?
- What does the storm reveal about the character that dialogue does not?
- How does the language describing the storm change as the character's emotions change?

## Key Terminology

When analysing weather as emotional landscape, use terms like: *pathetic fallacy, personification, turbulence, foreboding, mirroring, externalisation, atmospheric pressure, ominous, chaotic, relentless.*
