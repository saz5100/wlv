# AQA Marking Procedure

The AQA 8700/1 Sample Mark Scheme (June 2026) specifies a three-step marking procedure for examiners. This procedure ensures consistent and fair assessment across all responses.

## The Three-Step Method

### Step 1: Annotate the Response

Examiners should annotate the student's response with relevant comments, identifying:
- Evidence of skills from the skills descriptors
- Examples of the indicative standard
- Any features that support level determination

### Step 2: Determine the Level

Using the level descriptors and skills descriptors, examiners determine which level best fits the response. This involves:
- Matching the response to the level keywords (Perceptive/Detailed, Clear/Relevant, Some/Attempts, Simple/Limited)
- Considering the overall quality of the response
- Using professional judgement to place the response within the hierarchy

### Step 3: Determine the Mark

Within the chosen level, examiners decide on a specific mark by considering:
- Whether the response is at the top, middle, or bottom of the level
- The strength of evidence for the level descriptors
- The balance of strengths and weaknesses in the response

## Key Principles

- **Best fit approach:** The response is matched to the level that best describes it, even if not all descriptors are fully met
- **Holistic judgement:** Examiners consider the response as a whole rather than counting individual features
- **Consistency:** The same procedure is applied across all questions and all examiners
