# Environmental Oppression

Environmental oppression is a literary device in which the natural environment is depicted as a dominant, subjugating force that overwhelms and controls the characters. Unlike pathetic fallacy, where the environment reflects human emotions, environmental oppression presents nature as actively hostile or indifferent to human suffering.

## Key Characteristics

- **Nature as antagonist:** The environment is not a passive backdrop but an active force that causes physical and psychological distress.
- **Loss of human agency:** Characters are rendered powerless against the elements; their actions are reactive rather than proactive.
- **Sensory overload:** The environment assaults the senses — heat, sound, light — creating a feeling of inescapable pressure.
- **Psychological deterioration:** Prolonged exposure to the oppressive environment leads to mental decline.

## Example from *The Grass is Singing*

In the June 2024 extract, the heat is described as 'intolerable' and 'an obsession'. Mary is trapped in a cycle of trying to cool herself, but the environment offers no relief. The 'implacable sky' refuses to yield rain, and the 'sapping, undermining waves' of heat beat down from the iron roof. Mary's body and mind become 'subservient to the slow movement of the seasons', stripping her of autonomy.
