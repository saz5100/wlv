# AQA Q1 Retrieval Marking

Question 1 on AQA Paper 1 is a 4-mark explicit retrieval task. Students must identify four pieces of information from a specified section of the source text.

## Marking Rules

- **1 mark per point:** Four correct points = 4 marks.
- **Specified lines only:** Responses must be drawn from the lines specified in the question (e.g., lines 1-4).
- **Subject focus:** Responses must relate to the subject specified in the question (e.g., 'things about hyenas', not about Pi).
- **Paraphrasing allowed:** Students may paraphrase the text. Verbatim copying of individual phrases is acceptable.
- **No credit for whole-section copying:** Responses that copy the whole section of the text from the specified lines verbatim should not be credited any marks.
- **Indicative content is not exhaustive:** Markers must credit accurate, relevant responses not covered by the scheme.

## Indicative Content (June 2023 Example)

For the *Life of Pi* extract, the mark scheme lists over 20 possible correct responses about hyenas, including:

- They are ugly
- They have coarse hair
- They have shaggy coats
- They have spots
- They have a sloping back
- They have a bungled appearance
- They look like symptoms of a skin disease
- They are the size of a wolf
- They are a dog-like carnivore

## Self-Check for Markers

The mark scheme includes a self-check question: 'Does the response identify explicit information about hyenas from the specified lines?'
