# Comma Splice

A **comma splice** is a common grammatical error where a comma is used to connect two independent clauses (sentences) without a coordinating conjunction.

- **Incorrect (Splice)**: *"The weather was terrible, we decided to stay inside."*

## How to Fix a Comma Splice
There are three primary ways to resolve a comma splice:
1. **Use a Full Stop**: Create two separate sentences.
   - *"The weather was terrible. We decided to stay inside."*
2. **Use a Semicolon**: Connect the related ideas.
   - *"The weather was terrible; we decided to stay inside."*
3. **Use a Conjunction**: Add a coordinating conjunction (ASBO).
   - *"The weather was terrible, so we decided to stay inside."*
