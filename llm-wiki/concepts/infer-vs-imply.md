# Infer vs Imply

The distinction between 'infer' and 'imply' is a matter of directionality in communication. Confusing the two is a common linguistic pitfall.

## Imply (The Sender)
To **imply** is the act of hinting or suggesting something without stating it explicitly. This is the action performed by the **speaker** or **writer**.
- *Example*: "The teacher's tone implied that the students were not working hard enough."

## Infer (The Receiver)
To **infer** is the act of deducing or concluding something from evidence or suggestions. This is the action performed by the **reader** or **listener**.
- *Example*: "From the teacher's tone, the students inferred that they were not working hard enough."

In the context of AQA/WLV reading questions, students are asked to **infer** meaning from the writer's **implied** suggestions.
