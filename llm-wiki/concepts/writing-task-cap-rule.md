# Writing Task Cap Rule (AQA 2026)

The AQA 8700/1 Sample Mark Scheme (June 2026) introduces a task focus cap for Q5 (writing). If a candidate does not directly address the task focus, AO5 (Content and Organisation) is capped at the top of Level 2 (12 marks).

## How the Cap Works

- **Maximum AO5 mark:** 12 marks (top of Level 2)
- **Condition:** The cap applies only if the candidate does not directly address the task focus
- **AO6 unaffected:** The cap does not apply to AO6 (Technical Accuracy), which is marked independently

## Examples

- **On-task response:** A candidate who writes a description of a zoo or wildlife park (as instructed) is not capped
- **Off-task response:** A candidate who writes a story about a completely unrelated topic (e.g., a space adventure) is capped at 12 marks for AO5
- **Partially on-task response:** A candidate who addresses the task but with significant deviation may be capped depending on the extent of the deviation

## Implications for Students

- Students must read the writing prompt carefully and ensure they address the task directly
- The cap applies to both descriptive and narrative options
- Students should plan their response to ensure it stays focused on the task
