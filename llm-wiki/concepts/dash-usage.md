# Dash Usage

Dashes (—) are versatile punctuation marks used to create a break or shift in a sentence.

## Primary Uses
1. **Non-Essential Information**: Used in pairs to isolate a phrase, similar to brackets but with more emphasis.
   - *Example: "The cake—which took five hours to bake—was delicious."*
2. **Indicating a Shift**: Used to show a sudden change in thought or a dramatic pause.
   - *Example: "I opened the door and saw—nothing."*
