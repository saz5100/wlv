# Affect vs Effect

'Affect' and 'effect' are frequently confused due to their similar sounds, but they typically function as different parts of speech.

## Affect (Verb)
'Affect' is almost always a verb meaning to influence or produce a change in something.
- *Example*: "The loud noise affected her ability to concentrate."

## Effect (Noun)
'Effect' is almost always a noun referring to the result or consequence of an action.
- *Example*: "The medication had a positive effect on the patient."

## Rare Exception
'Effect' can occasionally be used as a verb meaning "to bring about" or "to implement" (e.g., "to effect change"). However, for the purposes of the WLV assessment, the verb/noun distinction is the primary rule to follow.
