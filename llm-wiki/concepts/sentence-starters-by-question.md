# Sentence Starters by Question

## Q1 — Retrieval (4 marks)

Keep it simple. Just list the facts:

- Four things I can see are:
- The text states that...
- According to the passage...
- The writer tells us that...

**Example response:**
1. The Master was slightly peculiar.
2. He had travelled abroad.
3. He owned a pet.
4. He lived alone.

## Q2 — Language Analysis (8 marks)

| Opening | Example |
|---------|---------|
| The writer uses [technique] to... | "The writer uses a simile to describe the sea as..." |
| The use of [word/phrase] conveys... | "The use of 'hammered' conveys the narrator's panic..." |
| This suggests / implies... | "This suggests the character feels trapped..." |
| The effect of this is to make the reader feel... | "The effect is to make the reader share the narrator's fear..." |
| The connotations of [word] are... | "The connotations of 'looming' suggest a threat that cannot be escaped..." |
| Furthermore / Moreover... | Adding a second point |
| This is reinforced by... | Linking two techniques together |

**Full paragraph structure:**
> "Firstly, the writer uses the simile 'like a trapped bird' to describe the narrator's heartbeat. This conveys a sense of desperation and panic. The word 'trapped' suggests helplessness, forcing the reader to empathise with the narrator's terror."

## Q3 — Structure Analysis (8 marks)

| Opening | Example |
|---------|---------|
| At the beginning of the text, the writer focuses on... | "At the beginning, the writer focuses on the calm setting..." |
| The writer then shifts the reader's attention to... | "The focus then shifts to the dark shape in the water..." |
| This contrast between [X] and [Y] highlights... | "This contrast between stillness and movement highlights the danger..." |
| The pace quickens when... | "The pace quickens as the paragraphs become shorter..." |
| The cyclical structure — returning to [image] — emphasises... | "The cyclical return to the beach image shows how the character has changed..." |

**Full paragraph structure:**
> "The writer structures the text to build tension. At first, long, calm sentences mirror the peaceful setting. Then, as danger approaches, the sentences become shorter and more fragmented: 'He squinted. It moved.' This abrupt shift jolts the reader."

## Q5 — Creative Writing (40 marks)

| Purpose | Starter |
|---------|---------|
| Wide shot opening | "The first thing that struck me was..." |
| Character introduction | "He was the kind of person who..." |
| Setting a mood | "The air was thick with..." |
| Sound focus | "The only sound was the relentless..." |
| Smell focus | "The smell of [x] hung in the air like..." |
| Sudden change | "Then, without warning..." |
| Building tension | "Every second stretched into an hour..." |
| Climax | "And then — [event]." |
| Resolution | "When it was over, all that remained was..." |

**Example opening:**
> "The air was thick with salt and the promise of rain. He stood at the water's edge, the waves lapping at his feet like a hungry creature tasting its next meal."

## General Connectives

| Purpose | Phrases |
|---------|---------|
| Adding a point | Moreover, Furthermore, In addition, Additionally |
| Contrasting | However, On the other hand, In contrast, Yet |
| Concluding | Overall, In conclusion, Ultimately, Therefore |
| Emphasising | Significantly, Notably, Crucially, Importantly |
