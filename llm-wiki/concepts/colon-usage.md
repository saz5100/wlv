# Colon Usage

A colon (:) acts as a "gateway," signaling that the information following it explains, expands upon, or lists what came before.

## Primary Uses
1. **Introducing a List**:
   - *Example: "You will need the following items: a pen, a ruler, and a calculator."*
2. **Introducing an Explanation or Expansion**:
   - *Example: "The reason for the delay was simple: the train had broken down."*
3. **Stylistic Emphasis**: Used to create a dramatic pause before a final word or phrase.

## Technical Requirement
A colon **cannot** be used if the preceding clause is not independent.
- **Incorrect**: *"I will: try my best."* (The part before the colon is not a complete sentence).
- **Correct**: *"I have a goal: I will try my best."*
