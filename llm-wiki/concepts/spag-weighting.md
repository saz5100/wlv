# SPaG Weighting

SPaG weighting refers to the proportion of writing marks allocated to spelling, punctuation, and grammar in the WLV English Equivalency test. Technical accuracy is worth 50% of the total writing score (12.5 out of 25 marks).

## Key Implications

- SPaG is not a minor component; it carries equal weight to content and organisation.
- Candidates must prioritise SPaG revision alongside creative writing skills.
- Common errors to address include comma splicing, apostrophe placement, and sentence demarcation.

## Recommended Focus Areas

- Comma splicing — understanding when to use full stops, semicolons, or conjunctions instead.
- Apostrophe of possession and contraction — correct placement and usage.
- Sentence demarcation — consistent use of capital letters and full stops.
- Punctuation variety — accurate use of colons, semicolons, dashes, and question marks.
