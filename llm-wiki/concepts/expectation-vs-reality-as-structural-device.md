# Expectation vs Reality as a Structural Device

## Definition

The contrast between a character’s expectation and the subsequent reality is a structural device that creates tension, dramatic irony, and thematic depth. The writer establishes an expectation (through a character’s thoughts, hopes, or plans) and then subverts it with the actual outcome, producing a shock that drives the narrative forward. This device is also referred to as *Expectativa vs Realidade*.

## Function in Narrative

- **Creation of Tension:** The reader anticipates the moment the character will confront the real situation.
- **Dramatic Irony:** The reader may perceive the discrepancy before the character does.
- **Character Development:** The character’s reaction to the shock reveals personality, resilience, and vulnerability.
- **Thematic Exploration:** The contrast can explore themes such as disillusionment, loss, social class, or the nature of happiness.
- **Structural Pacing:** In AQA Paper 1, the device often appears as a flashback (to establish expectation) followed by a return to the present (to reveal reality), creating a clear structural pivot.

## Example in *The Glass House* (November 2024)

Rita’s expectation of Foxcote Manor is established through the flashback to the interview, where she “gazed up at the house’s sugar-white walls” with admiration, idealising a London family like the Darlings from *Peter Pan*. The reality, however, is a rundown house: “windows blink drunkenly” and a roof that “sags in the middle, like a snapped spine”. This contrast between the imagined perfect house and the decaying Foxcote Manor is the emotional engine of the extract, driving the evaluation statement “Rita’s dream becomes a nightmare.”

## How to Analyse This Device (for Q3 and Q4)

1. **Identify the Expectation:** What does the character hope for or imagine? (e.g., Rita expects a grand, elegant mansion.)
2. **Identify the Reality:** What is the actual situation the character encounters? (e.g., Foxcote Manor is a dilapidated ruin.)
3. **Analyse the Contrast:** How does the writer emphasise the difference? Look for:
   - Language (figurative imagery, sensory details, juxtaposition of positive vs. negative diction)
   - Structure (flashback vs. present tense, temporal shifts, paragraph breaks that signal a change)
4. **Examine the Impact:** How does this contrast affect the character (emotional response, change in attitude) and the reader (empathy, suspense, thematic insight)?
5. **Consider Writer’s Intention:** Why did the writer choose to present this contrast? What effect on the overall message or evaluation statement?
