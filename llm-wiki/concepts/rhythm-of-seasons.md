# Rhythm of Seasons

The rhythm of seasons is a structural and thematic device in which the cyclical pattern of the natural world governs the lives of characters. It is often used to contrast human agency with the implacable forces of nature.

## Function in Literature

- **Structural framework:** The progression of seasons provides a natural chronological structure for a narrative.
- **Thematic contrast:** The regularity of seasons can be juxtaposed with the chaos or stagnation of human life.
- **Loss of control:** Characters who are 'subservient' to the seasons are stripped of autonomy, reinforcing themes of powerlessness.

## Example from *The Grass is Singing*

In the June 2024 extract, Mary reflects that in town 'there had been no seasons, really', whereas on the farm 'body and mind were subservient to the slow movement of the seasons'. This contrast highlights her loss of agency and her alienation from the natural world. She is forced to 'watch the implacable sky for signs of rain', a passive and anxious activity that underscores her powerlessness.
