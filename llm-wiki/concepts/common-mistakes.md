# Common Mistakes

Common mistakes are recurring errors that students make in examination responses. Identifying and understanding these mistakes is a key revision strategy, as it allows candidates to avoid predictable pitfalls and maximise their marks.

## Sources

The [[search-lan-research-log]] identified three dedicated resources on common mistakes:
- **First Rate Tutors:** 'Common Mistakes in English Language Paper 1'
- **Tutopiya:** 'AQA GCSE English Language Common Mistakes List'
- **Richard Lander School:** 'AQA Language Supporting Booklet 2024' (includes sentence stems and tips)

These sources have been synthesised into the [[common-mistakes-compilation]] page.

## Categories of Common Mistakes

### Content Mistakes
- Misinterpreting the question or prompt
- Including irrelevant material
- Failing to address all parts of the question

### Structural Mistakes
- Poor planning leading to disorganised responses
- Weak openings and endings
- Lack of paragraph cohesion

### Analytical Mistakes
- Feature-spotting without analysis (naming devices without explaining effects)
- Retelling the story instead of analysing
- Weak or irrelevant evidence selection

### Technical Mistakes (SPaG)
- Comma splicing
- Incorrect apostrophe usage (possession and contraction)
- Poor sentence demarcation
- Inconsistent tense
- Spelling errors (especially homophones)

### Timing Mistakes
- Spending too long on one question
- Rushing the final question
- Not leaving time for proofreading

## Why Common Mistakes Matter for WLV Candidates

- **SPaG weighting:** 50% of writing marks in the WLV test are allocated to technical accuracy. SPaG mistakes are therefore more costly than in AQA.
- **Single-source extraction:** All reading questions use one extract; mistakes in Q1 (not reading line references) are especially important.
- **Fixed thematic prompt:** There is no writing choice; ignoring the prompt is a critical error.
- **Condensed format:** The 2-hour format means timing mistakes are harder to recover from.

## How to Use Common Mistakes Resources

1. **Diagnose your own weaknesses:** Read through the common mistakes list and identify which ones apply to your own writing.
2. **Targeted practice:** Focus revision on the specific mistakes you make most often.
3. **Proofreading checklist:** Use the common mistakes list as a proofreading checklist before submitting any practice response.
4. **Cross-reference with SPaG reference:** For technical mistakes, consult the [[spag-reference]] page for rules and correction strategies.
