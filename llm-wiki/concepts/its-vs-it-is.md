# Its vs It's

The confusion between 'its' and 'it's' is a common error involving the intersection of possessive pronouns and contractions.

## Its (Possessive)
'Its' is a possessive pronoun. Like 'his' or 'hers', it does **not** take an apostrophe to show ownership.
- *Example*: "The dog wagged **its** tail."

## It's (Contraction)
'It's' is a contraction of "it is" or "it has". The apostrophe marks the omission of a letter (elision).
- *Example*: "**It's** raining outside" (It is raining).

**Quick Tip**: If you can replace the word with "it is", use the apostrophe. If you cannot, do not use the apostrophe.
