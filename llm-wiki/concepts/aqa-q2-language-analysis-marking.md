# AQA Q2 Language Analysis Marking

Question 2 on AQA Paper 1 is an 8-mark language analysis task assessing AO2. Students must analyse how the writer uses language to achieve effects and influence the reader.

## Four-Level Descriptors

| Level | Descriptor | Marks |
|-------|------------|-------|
| Level 4 | Perceptive, Detailed | 7-8 |
| Level 3 | Clear, Relevant | 5-6 |
| Level 2 | Some, Attempts | 3-4 |
| Level 1 | Simple, Limited | 1-2 |

## Key Marking Principles

- **Quality over quantity:** It is the quality of comments, not the number of references, that determines the level.
- **Implicit methods:** Reference to writer's methods may be implicit without specific mention of technical terminology.
- **Indicative content is not exhaustive:** Markers must credit accurate, relevant responses not covered by the scheme.

## Indicative Content (June 2023 Example)

For the *Life of Pi* extract (lines 10-19), the mark scheme identifies the following language features:

- **Adjectives:** 'ugly', 'shaggy', 'coarse', 'bungled'
- **Verbs:** 'bungled' (used as a verb)
- **Contrast:** Between the hyena's appearance and other animals
- **Simile:** 'like the symptoms of a skin disease'
- **Adverbs:** Used to modify descriptions
- **Humour/Hyperbole:** Exaggerated descriptions for effect
- **Short declarative sentences:** For emphasis
- **Antithesis:** Contrasting ideas within the same sentence
