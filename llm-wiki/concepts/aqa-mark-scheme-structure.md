# AQA Mark Scheme Structure

The AQA GCSE English Language Paper 1 mark scheme uses a consistent four-level hierarchy across all questions. This structure is designed to allow markers to place responses into broad levels of achievement before fine-tuning within each level.

## The Four Levels

| Level | Descriptor | Marks (8-mark Q) | Marks (20-mark Q) | Marks (24-mark Q) |
|-------|------------|------------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Level 4 | Perceptive, Detailed | 7-8 | 16-20 | 19-24 |
| Level 3 | Clear, Relevant | 5-6 | 11-15 | 13-18 |
| Level 2 | Some, Attempts | 3-4 | 6-10 | 7-12 |
| Level 1 | Simple, Limited | 1-2 | 1-5 | 1-6 |

## Key Principles

- **Best-fit approach:** Markers place responses into the level that best describes the overall quality, not by counting features.
- **Quality over quantity:** For analytical questions (Q2, Q3), it is the quality of comments, not the number of references, that determines the level.
- **Indicative content is not exhaustive:** The mark scheme lists possible responses, but markers must credit accurate, relevant responses not covered by the scheme.
- **Implicit understanding:** Reference to writer's methods may be implicit without specific mention of technical terminology.

## Progression Keywords

The levels are defined by progression keywords that describe increasing sophistication:

- **Level 1:** Simple, limited
- **Level 2:** Some, attempts
- **Level 3:** Clear, relevant
- **Level 4:** Perceptive, detailed
