# Heat as Obsession

Heat as obsession is a literary device in which extreme temperature is presented not merely as a physical condition but as a consuming psychological force that dominates a character's thoughts, actions, and emotional state. The term is drawn from the AQA June 2024 Paper 1 extract from Doris Lessing's *The Grass is Singing*, where the narrator states that 'the heat became an obsession' for the protagonist Mary Turner.

## Key Features

- **Psychological dominance:** The heat occupies the character's mind to the exclusion of other concerns.
- **Sensory saturation:** The text uses vivid sensory details (sight, sound, touch) to immerse the reader in the experience.
- **Physical manifestation:** The heat causes tangible physical effects — panting, whining, hissing — that reinforce its oppressive presence.
- **Environmental oppression:** The landscape itself becomes an antagonist, actively working against the character's comfort and wellbeing.

## Examples from the Extract

- 'the heat became an obsession'
- 'she could not bear the sapping, undermining waves that beat down from the iron roof'
- 'the scattering drops fell on the porous brick, which hissed with dryness'

## Analytical Application

When analysing heat as obsession in a text, consider:

1. **Lexical field:** What words are associated with heat? (e.g., 'waves', 'beat', 'sapping', 'undermining')
2. **Personification:** Is the heat given human or animal qualities? (e.g., 'beat up', 'sapping')
3. **Sensory accumulation:** How does the writer layer sensory details to build intensity?
4. **Structural progression:** Does the focus on heat intensify or shift as the text develops?
