# AQA Assessment Objectives

The AQA English Language specification is governed by specific Assessment Objectives (AOs) that determine how marks are allocated across the reading and writing sections.

## Reading Objectives
- **AO1**: Identify and interpret explicit and implicit information and ideas. (Primary focus of Q1).
- **AO2**: Analyse the language, form, and structure used by a writer to create effects. (Primary focus of Q2 and Q3).
- **AO4**: Evaluate texts critically, interpreting information and ideas. (Primary focus of Q4).

## Writing Objectives
- **AO5**: Communicate clearly, effectively, and imaginatively, selecting and adapting tone, style, and register for different purposes and audiences.
- **AO6**: Use a range of vocabulary and sentence structures for clarity, purpose, and effect, with accurate spelling and punctuation.

## WLV Context
In the WLV Equivalency test, the focus is heavily weighted towards **AO1**, **AO2**, and **AO6**. AO4 is removed from the assessment scope.
