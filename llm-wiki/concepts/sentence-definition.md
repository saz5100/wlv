# Sentence Definition

A sentence is a group of words that makes complete sense. To be grammatically complete, it must meet three criteria:
1. It must contain a **main verb** (a finite verb).
2. It must start with a **capital letter**.
3. It must end with a terminal punctuation mark: a **full stop**, **exclamation mark**, or **question mark**.

*Note: In creative writing, authors may intentionally use "sentence fragments" (incomplete sentences) for stylistic effect, but this is a conscious choice rather than a grammatical error.*
