# Sentence Demarcation

Sentence demarcation refers to the ability to correctly identify and mark the boundaries between sentences. This is a fundamental component of **AO6 (Technical Accuracy)** and is a primary differentiator between marking levels.

## Importance in Assessment
In the AQA and WLV marking frameworks, the security of sentence demarcation often determines the ceiling of a student's mark:

- **Level 4 (Consistently Secure)**: Sentence boundaries are marked accurately and consistently throughout the piece. There is no evidence of comma splicing or run-on sentences.
- **Level 3 (Generally Secure)**: Most sentence boundaries are correct, though occasional errors may occur that do not impede communication.
- **Level 2 (Some Control)**: Basic sentence boundaries are marked, but there are frequent errors (e.g., comma splices) that may occasionally confuse the reader.
- **Level 1 (Limited Control)**: Frequent errors in demarcation; sentences may run together or be fragmented, significantly hindering clarity.

## Common Errors to Avoid
- **Comma Splicing**: Joining two independent clauses with only a comma.
- **Run-on Sentences**: Failing to use any punctuation between independent clauses.
- **Fragmented Sentences**: Leaving a clause without a subject or verb, creating an incomplete thought (unless used intentionally for stylistic effect).
