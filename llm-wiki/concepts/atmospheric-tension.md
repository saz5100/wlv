# Atmospheric Tension

Atmospheric tension refers to the deliberate creation of a high-stress or oppressive environment within a text to evoke anxiety or anticipation in the reader. In the context of AQA Paper 1, this is often a primary focus for Question 3 (Structural Analysis).

## Techniques for Creating Tension
- **Pacing:** Using repetitive actions or rhythmic descriptions to build a sense of inevitability. In the [[life-of-pi-extract-june-2023]], the hyena's repetitive laps ("one lap-two-three-four-five") create a ticking-clock effect.
- **Sensory Details:** Focusing on intrusive sounds or sights that signal danger. Examples include the "clicking" of claws and the high-pitched "yip yip yip" of the hyena.
- **Claustrophobia:** Limiting the physical space of the characters (e.g., the "twenty-five-foot indoor track" of the lifeboat) to increase the feeling of entrapment.
- **Internal Monologue:** Contrasting the character's hope or denial with the external reality of the threat.

## Application in Analysis
When analysing atmospheric tension, students should identify:
1. **The Trigger:** What starts the tension?
2. **The Escalation:** How does the writer increase the pressure?
3. **The Release:** How is the tension broken (e.g., through an [[anticlimax]])?
