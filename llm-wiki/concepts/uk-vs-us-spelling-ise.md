# UK vs US Spelling (-ise)

A primary distinction between British and American English is the suffix used for certain verbs.

## The Rule of Thumb
- **British English**: Uses the **-ise** ending (e.g., *analyse*, *categorise*, *organise*).
- **American English**: Uses the **-ize** ending (e.g., *analyze*, *categorize*, *organize*).

For the WLV English Equivalency test, students must strictly adhere to British English spelling conventions.
