# AO5: Content and Organisation

Assessment Objective 5 (AO5) focuses on the writer's ability to communicate their ideas clearly, imaginatively, and coherently. In the context of AQA Paper 1 Question 5, this objective assesses the "what" and "how" of the creative piece.

## Assessment Criteria
The quality of AO5 is judged on a four-level scale:

- **Level 4 (Perceptive/Compelling)**: Characterised by compelling communication, ambitious vocabulary, and seamlessly integrated discourse markers. The writing is highly effective in its tone, style, and register.
- **Level 3 (Clear/Consistent)**: Clear and coherent communication with a consistent tone and appropriate organisation.
- **Level 2 (Some/Attempts)**: Some attempt at organisation; communication is generally clear but may lack sophistication or consistency.
- **Level 1 (Simple/Limited)**: Simple communication with limited organisation and basic vocabulary.

## Key Focus Areas
- **Tone and Register**: The ability to adapt the voice of the piece to suit the purpose (e.g., a descriptive piece vs. a narrative story).
- **Cohesion**: The use of structural features and discourse markers to link ideas and paragraphs.
- **Vocabulary**: The use of precise and evocative language to create atmosphere or character.
