# Phrase Types

A phrase is a meaningful unit of words that does not contain a finite verb and therefore cannot be a clause.

## Common Phrase Types
- **Noun Phrase**: A noun and its modifiers (e.g., *"The big, red bus"*).
- **Verb Phrase**: A main verb and its auxiliaries (e.g., *"has been running"*).
- **Adjective Phrase**: An adjective and its modifiers (e.g., *"very bright"*).
- **Adverbial Phrase**: An adverb and its modifiers (e.g., *"quite slowly"*).
- **Prepositional Phrase**: A preposition followed by a noun phrase (e.g., *"on the table"*).
