# Oxford Comma

The **Oxford Comma** (or serial comma) is the optional comma placed immediately before the coordinating conjunction (usually 'and' or 'or') in a list of three or more items.

- **Example without Oxford Comma**: *"I like apples, oranges and bananas."*
- **Example with Oxford Comma**: *"I like apples, oranges, and bananas."*

While optional in many UK styles, it is often used to prevent ambiguity in complex lists.
