# Model Answer

A model answer is an exemplar response that demonstrates the expected quality, structure, and technique required to achieve a specific grade level in an examination. In the context of AQA Paper 1 and the WLV English Equivalency test, model answers serve as benchmarks for self-assessment and as teaching tools for deconstructing effective exam technique.

## Purpose

- **Benchmarking:** Provides a concrete example of what a Grade 9 (or equivalent top-band) response looks like.
- **Technique deconstruction:** Allows students to analyse how successful responses are structured, how evidence is selected, and how analysis is developed.
- **Self-assessment:** Enables students to compare their own responses against a standard and identify areas for improvement.
- **Modelling:** Teachers and tutors can use model answers to demonstrate specific skills (e.g., embedding quotations, using the what-how-why framework).

## How to Use Model Answers

1. **Read the question first:** Understand what is being asked before looking at the model answer.
2. **Deconstruct the response:** Identify the structure, evidence selection, analytical framework, and technical accuracy.
3. **Annotate:** Mark where the response meets specific mark scheme criteria (e.g., Level 4 descriptors).
4. **Compare with your own:** Write your own response to the same question, then compare it against the model answer.
5. **Focus on technique, not memorisation:** Model answers are for learning *how* to write, not for memorising content.

## WLV-Specific Considerations

- Model answers from AQA resources assume the full Paper 1 format (including Q4 and writing choice). WLV candidates should focus on Q2, Q3, and Q5 model answers.
- The WLV Equivalency test uses a fixed thematic prompt for Q5; model answers that assume choice should be used for technique only.
- Model answers may not reflect the WLV SPaG weighting (50% of writing marks). Candidates should pay particular attention to technical accuracy in their own responses.
