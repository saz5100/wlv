# Level Keywords for AQA Marking

The AQA 8700/1 Sample Mark Scheme (June 2026) establishes a consistent four-level hierarchy across all questions. These level keywords are used in skills descriptors and indicative standards to differentiate student performance.

## The Four-Level Hierarchy

| Level | Keyword | Description |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| Level 4 | Perceptive, detailed | Shows sophisticated understanding and analysis |
| Level 3 | Clear, relevant | Shows competent understanding with relevant points |
| Level 2 | Some, attempts | Shows partial understanding with limited attempts |
| Level 1 | Simple, limited | Shows basic understanding with minimal detail |

## Application Across Questions

- **Q1 (AO1):** Multiple-choice — levels apply to overall performance across the four questions
- **Q2 (AO2):** Language analysis — levels describe depth of analysis of words, phrases, and language features
- **Q3 (AO2):** Structural analysis — levels describe depth of analysis of structural features
- **Q4 (AO4):** Evaluation — levels describe quality of evaluation and critical understanding
- **Q5 (AO5):** Content and organisation — levels describe quality of communication and structure
- **Q6 (AO6):** Technical accuracy — levels describe accuracy of spelling, punctuation, and grammar

## Progression Keywords

The levels use a progression of keywords to indicate increasing sophistication:

- **Level 1:** Simple, limited, basic
- **Level 2:** Some, attempts, straightforward
- **Level 3:** Clear, relevant, effective
- **Level 4:** Perceptive, detailed, sophisticated, convincing
