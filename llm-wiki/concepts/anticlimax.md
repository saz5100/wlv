# Anticlimax

An anticlimax is a structural device where a period of high tension or a build-up toward a significant event ends in a disappointing, abrupt, or trivial manner. It is often used to shift the tone of a piece or to subvert the reader's expectations.

## Structural Function
In narrative architecture, an anticlimax serves to:
- **Subvert Expectations:** The reader expects a violent confrontation, but receives a mundane or repulsive event instead.
- **Shift Tone:** Transitioning from a mood of terror to one of disgust or irony.
- **Highlight Absurdity:** Emphasising the unpredictability of a situation or character.

## Example: *Life of Pi* (June 2023 Extract)
The extract builds significant tension as the hyena runs in circles and eventually fixes its gaze on Pi. The narrator explicitly states, "I prepared for my end." However, the tension is broken not by an attack, but by the hyena "hacking and coughing" and abruptly vomiting. This shifts the scene from a life-threatening encounter to a display of "animal anguish" and physical repulsion.
