# AQA Q3 Structural Analysis Marking

Question 3 on AQA Paper 1 is an 8-mark structural analysis task assessing AO2. Students must analyse how the writer structures the text to interest the reader.

## Four-Level Descriptors

| Level | Descriptor | Marks |
|-------|------------|-------|
| Level 4 | Perceptive, Detailed | 7-8 |
| Level 3 | Clear, Relevant | 5-6 |
| Level 2 | Some, Attempts | 3-4 |
| Level 1 | Simple, Limited | 1-2 |

## Key Marking Principles

- **Whole text analysis:** Students should consider the structure of the whole source, not just a single section.
- **Quality over quantity:** It is the quality of comments, not the number of references, that determines the level.
- **Implicit methods:** Reference to writer's methods may be implicit without specific mention of technical terminology.

## Indicative Content (June 2023 Example)

For the *Life of Pi* extract, the mark scheme identifies the following structural features:

- **Exposition:** Introduction of the setting and characters
- **Foreshadowing:** Hints of danger to come
- **Focus shifts:** Movement between different elements of the scene
- **First person perspective:** Pi's viewpoint shaping the reader's experience
- **Motifs:** Recurring images or ideas (e.g., circling)
- **Rising action:** Building tension towards a climax
- **Juxtaposition:** Contrasting elements placed side by side
- **Internal v external:** Contrast between Pi's internal thoughts and external events
- **Anti-climax:** The threat diminishes rather than reaching a dramatic peak
