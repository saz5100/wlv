# Level of Response Marking

The "Level of Response" system is a tiered marking framework used by AQA to categorise the quality of a student's analysis. Rather than a simple checklist of points, examiners look for the *quality* of the insight.

## The Four-Level Hierarchy

| Level | Descriptor | Characteristics |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Level 4** | **Perceptive** | Detailed, nuanced analysis; understands the psychological or narrative effect of the writer's choices. |
| **Level 3** | **Clear** | Relevant and clear explanations; identifies techniques and explains their general effect. |
| **Level 2** | **Some** | Attempts at analysis; identifies some features but explanations may be limited or superficial. |
| **Level 1** | **Simple** | Limited response; simple identification of points with little to no analysis of effect. |

## Application in Analysis
To move from Level 3 (Clear) to Level 4 (Perceptive), a student must move beyond identifying *what* a technique is (e.g., "this is a simile") to explaining *why* it was used and *how* it specifically manipulates the reader's perception or reveals a character's internal state.
