# Exam Timing and Strategy

Exam timing and strategy refers to the allocation of time and the sequence of actions a candidate follows during an examination. Effective timing is critical for maximising marks across all questions.

## AQA Paper 1 Timing (1 hour 45 minutes)

The [[search-lan-research-log]] identified Save My Exams as a source for AQA Paper 1 timing guidance. The standard AQA recommendation is:

| Question | Marks | Recommended Time |
|----------|-------|------------------|
| Q1 — Explicit Retrieval | 4 | 5 minutes |
| Q2 — Language Analysis | 8 | 10–12 minutes |
| Q3 — Structure Analysis | 8 | 10–12 minutes |
| Q4 — Evaluation | 20 | 20–25 minutes |
| Q5 — Creative Writing | 40 | 45 minutes |
| **Total** | **80** | **1 hour 45 minutes** |

## WLV Equivalency Test Timing (2 hours)

The WLV Equivalency test is 2 hours (15 minutes longer than AQA Paper 1) but removes Q4 and writing choice. The recommended timing for WLV candidates is:

| Question | Marks | Recommended Time |
|----------|-------|------------------|
| Q1 — Explicit Retrieval | 4 | 5 minutes |
| Q2 — Language Analysis | 8 | 15 minutes |
| Q3 — Structure Analysis | 8 | 15 minutes |
| Q5 — Creative Writing | 40 | 60 minutes |
| **Buffer/Proofreading** | — | 25 minutes |
| **Total** | **60** | **2 hours** |

## Key Strategic Considerations

### Reading Time
- Spend the first 10–15 minutes reading the extract carefully before attempting any questions.
- Annotate the extract as you read: underline key language features, note structural shifts, and identify potential evidence for Q2 and Q3.

### Question Order
- Answer questions in order (Q1 → Q2 → Q3 → Q5). This builds confidence and ensures you do not miss any questions.
- Do not skip Q1 — it is straightforward retrieval and earns easy marks.

### Q5 Timing
- Allocate the largest block of time to Q5 (40–60 minutes depending on the test format).
- Break Q5 time into: planning (5–10 minutes), writing (30–40 minutes), proofreading (5–10 minutes).

### Proofreading
- Always leave time to proofread your Q5 response for SPaG errors.
- In the WLV test, SPaG accounts for 50% of writing marks — proofreading is essential.

## WLV-Specific Adaptations

- **No Q4:** The time saved by removing Q4 (20–25 minutes in AQA) should be redistributed to Q5 and proofreading.
- **No writing choice:** You do not need to spend time deciding between prompts; the prompt is fixed.
- **Single-source extraction:** All reading questions use one extract; you only need to read and annotate one text.
- **2-hour format:** The extra 15 minutes compared to AQA should be used for careful reading and thorough proofreading.

## Common Timing Mistakes

- Spending too long on Q2 or Q3 (over-analysing a single quotation)
- Rushing Q5 due to poor time management earlier in the exam
- Not leaving time for proofreading
- Spending excessive time planning Q5 (more than 10 minutes)
- Reading the extract too quickly and missing key details
