# Subordinating Conjunctions

Subordinating conjunctions are connectives used to introduce a dependent (subordinate) clause. They link the subordinate clause to the main clause, often establishing a relationship of time, cause, or condition.

## Common Examples
- **Cause**: *because*, *since*, *as*.
- **Condition**: *if*, *unless*, *provided that*.
- **Contrast**: *although*, *even though*, *whereas*.
- **Time**: *while*, *when*, *before*, *after*, *until*.
