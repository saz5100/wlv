# Gender-Inclusive Marking Policy (AQA 2026)

The AQA 8700/1 Sample Mark Scheme (June 2026) formalises a gender-inclusive marking policy. This policy confirms that the use of 'they/them' as a singular pronoun and pronouns beyond 'she/her' or 'he/him' will be credited in exam responses.

## Key Points

- **Singular 'they/them':** The use of 'they/them' as a singular pronoun is accepted and credited
- **Pronoun diversity:** Pronouns beyond 'she/her' or 'he/him' (e.g., 'ze/zir') are accepted and credited
- **No penalty:** Students will not be penalised for using gender-inclusive language
- **Consistency:** The policy applies across all questions and all responses

## Implications for Students

- Students can use gender-inclusive language without fear of losing marks
- The policy supports diverse expression and identity
- Students should focus on the quality of their analysis and writing, not on avoiding gender-inclusive language
