# Digital Literacy

## Digital Skills for University

### Digital Tools
- **Learning management systems**: Using platforms like Canvas, Moodle, and Blackboard effectively
- **Productivity software**: Word processing, spreadsheets, presentations, and note-taking applications
- **Collaboration tools**: Using shared documents, project management platforms, and virtual whiteboards
- **Specialist software**: Discipline-specific tools for data analysis, design, and research
- **Cloud computing**: Storing, sharing, and accessing files through cloud services

### Information Literacy
- **Search strategies**: Using advanced search techniques and academic databases effectively
- **Source evaluation**: Assessing credibility, authority, and reliability of digital information
- **Open access resources**: Finding and using open educational resources and open-access journals
- **Digital libraries**: Navigating online catalogues, repositories, and archives
- **Data management**: Organising, storing, and backing up digital research data

### Digital Communication
- **Email etiquette**: Writing professional emails for academic and professional contexts
- **Virtual meetings**: Participating effectively in video conferences and online seminars
- **Online discussion forums**: Contributing constructively to academic discussions
- **Social media for learning**: Using professional networks like LinkedIn and academic Twitter
- **Digital collaboration**: Working effectively in virtual teams and shared online spaces

### Digital Safety
- **Cybersecurity**: Protecting passwords, devices, and personal information online
- **Data protection**: Understanding GDPR and your rights regarding personal data
- **Digital footprint**: Managing your online presence and professional reputation
- **Copyright and licensing**: Understanding intellectual property rights and Creative Commons
- **Critical digital citizenship**: Recognising misinformation, disinformation, and online manipulation
