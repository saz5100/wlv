# Mary Turner

Mary Turner is the central protagonist of Doris Lessing's 1950 novel *The Grass is Singing*. The extract used in the AQA June 2024 Paper 1 examination focuses on her experience of extreme heat and her strained relationship with her husband, Dick Turner.

## Character Profile

Mary is portrayed as a woman suffering under the oppressive heat of the Southern African landscape. The extract describes her obsessive preoccupation with the heat, her physical discomfort, and her attempts to find relief. She is presented as isolated and powerless within her marriage, with Dick's anger directed towards her.

## Key Quotations from the Extract

- 'the heat became an obsession'
- 'she could not bear the sapping, undermining waves that beat down from the iron roof'
- 'she stood in a basin on the brick floor, pouring it over her'

## Thematic Significance

Mary's character embodies themes of environmental oppression, psychological breakdown, and marital conflict. The heat functions as both a literal and metaphorical force, reflecting her internal state of distress and entrapment.
