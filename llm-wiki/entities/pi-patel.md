# Pi Patel (Piscine Molitor Patel)

Pi Patel is the protagonist and first-person narrator of Yann Martel's novel *Life of Pi* (2001). In the extract used for the AQA Paper 1 June 2023 examination, Pi is stranded on a lifeboat in the Pacific Ocean after a shipwreck and observes a spotted hyena that has also found its way onto the boat.

## Role in the Extract

Pi serves as the observer and commentator. His narrative voice is characterised by a blend of scientific detachment, philosophical reflection, and vivid, often humorous description. He explicitly states, 'I am not one to hold a prejudice against any animal,' before proceeding to deliver an intensely negative description of the hyena's appearance.

## Narrative Perspective

The extract is written in **first-person limited narration** from Pi's perspective. The reader sees the hyena entirely through Pi's eyes, filtered through his cultural and personal biases. This narrative mode is central to the extract's tone and effect.
