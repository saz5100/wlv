# Spotted Hyena

The spotted hyena (*Crocuta crocuta*) is the central subject of the extract from *Life of Pi* used in the AQA Paper 1 June 2023 examination. In the extract, the hyena arrives on Pi's lifeboat and is described in intensely pejorative terms before behaving in a wild, erratic manner.

## Role in the Extract

The hyena functions as both a literal threat and a symbolic presence. Pi's description emphasises its ugliness and apparent incompetence, creating a tension between the hyena as a dangerous predator and as a ridiculous, almost pathetic creature. This tension is the basis for the Q4 evaluation statement ('funny rather than frightening').

## Key Descriptive Features

- **Appearance**: 'ugly beyond redemption', 'shaggy, coarse coat', 'bungled mix of colours', 'spots like symptoms of a skin disease', 'mouse-like ears', 'scraggly and unwagging tail'
- **Behaviour**: Wild, erratic, panting, with an 'open and panting' mouth
- **Comparison**: 'like no dog anyone would want as a pet'
