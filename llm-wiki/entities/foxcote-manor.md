# Foxcote Manor

Foxcote Manor is the primary setting in the extract from *The Glass House* by Eve Chase (used in the November 2024 AQA Paper 1). It is the country house to which the Harrington family moves following a fire in London.

## Description

The manor is depicted as a rundown and neglected estate, described as a "wrecked beauty" that "erupts from the undergrowth." Its appearance is decadent and threatening, characterized by:

- **Structural Decay:** A red-tiled roof that "sags in the middle, like a snapped spine," suggesting a loss of control and instability.
- **Distorted Architecture:** Chimneys leaning at strange angles and windows that "blink drunkenly."
- **Overwhelming Nature:** Dense, vibrant ivy covered in birds and a "billowing veil of bees."

## Symbolism and Function

Foxcote Manor serves as a stark contrast to the "sugar-white walls" of the idealized London dream and symbolizes the ruin of Rita's expectations.

- **Expectation vs. Reality:** Instead of the elegant, perfect home she imagined, Rita finds a house in ruins being swallowed by nature, representing a "nightmare" reality.
- **Emotional Reflection:** The house functions as a reflection of Jeannie's emotional state (fragility, decadence) and Rita's internal fears (oppression, danger).
- **Atmosphere:** The setting contributes to a pervasive sense of foreboding and disappointment.
