# Doris Lessing

Doris Lessing (1919–2013) was a British-Zimbabwean novelist, poet, playwright, and short story writer. She was awarded the Nobel Prize in Literature in 2007. Her work spans a wide range of genres and themes, including colonialism, psychology, social realism, and science fiction.

## Relevance to the Wiki

Lessing's 1950 novel *The Grass is Singing* is the source text for the AQA June 2024 Paper 1 examination. The extract used in the exam focuses on the protagonist Mary Turner's experience of extreme heat and her strained marriage to Dick Turner. The novel is set in Southern Africa (now Zimbabwe) and explores themes of racial tension, environmental oppression, and psychological breakdown.

## Key Works

- *The Grass is Singing* (1950)
- *The Golden Notebook* (1962)
- *The Children of Violence* series (1952–1969)
- *The Memoirs of a Survivor* (1974)
- *The Fifth Child* (1988)

## Style and Themes

Lessing's prose is characterised by its psychological depth, sensory detail, and social commentary. In *The Grass is Singing*, she uses the oppressive heat of the African landscape as both a physical and psychological force, reflecting the internal turmoil of her characters. Her writing often employs third-person limited narration, focusing closely on a single character's perspective.
