# The Grass is Singing

*The Grass is Singing* is the debut novel of British-Zimbabwean author Doris Lessing, first published in 1950. The novel is set in Southern Africa (now Zimbabwe) and explores themes of racial tension, environmental oppression, psychological breakdown, and marital conflict.

## Relevance to the Wiki

An extract from *The Grass is Singing* serves as Source A for the AQA June 2024 Paper 1 examination. The extract focuses on the protagonist Mary Turner's experience of extreme heat and her strained relationship with her husband, Dick Turner. The text provides rich material for language analysis (Q2) and structural analysis (Q3).

## Extract Summary

The extract is taken from the middle of the novel. It depicts Mary's obsessive preoccupation with the oppressive heat, her physical discomfort, and her attempts to find relief. The second part of the extract (lines 18 to end) shifts focus to Dick's anger towards Mary, revealing the power dynamics of their marriage.

## Key Themes

- Environmental oppression and the psychological effects of extreme heat
- Marital conflict and powerlessness
- Isolation and psychological breakdown
- Colonial setting and its impact on characters

## Literary Style

Lessing employs third-person limited narration, focusing closely on Mary's perspective. The prose is rich in sensory detail, using vivid imagery of heat, light, and sound to create an atmosphere of oppression and discomfort.
