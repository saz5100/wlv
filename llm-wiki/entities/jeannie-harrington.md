# Jeannie Harrington

**Jeannie Harrington** is a character in *The Glass House*. She is the wife of Walter Harrington and the mother of the children for whom Rita provides care. In the narrative, she is portrayed as a fragile, broken, and wounded woman who is reluctant to be at Foxcote Manor.

## Physical and Emotional Description

Jeannie's appearance reflects her internal distress and trauma:
- **Physical Decline:** It is "painfully obvious" that she has recently lost weight, noted by how her cream crepe skirt is "tightly belted."
- **Emotional Masking:** She wears large sunglasses to hide the effects of "a night of crying."
- **Defensive Posture:** She is described as "clutching a black patent handbag, as if for dear life."
- **Symbolism of Trauma:** Her clothing is described as a "bandage" around her neck, further suggesting vulnerability and trauma.

## Narrative Function

Jeannie serves as a critical structural device to highlight the gap between expectation and reality:

- **Contrast to Idealization:** She represents the stark reality that contrasts with Rita's idealizations. Instead of the "perfect" and "delicate" mother figure Rita had imagined, Jeannie is a broken woman.
- **Atmospheric Tension:** Her silent presence and her refusal to participate in the move to Foxcote Manor increase the overall tension and discomfort within the excerpt.
