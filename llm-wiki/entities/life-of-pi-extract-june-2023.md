# Life of Pi Extract (June 2023)

The source text for the AQA Paper 1 June 2023 examination is an extract from *Life of Pi* (2001) by Yann Martel. The extract is set in the middle of the novel and depicts the protagonist, Pi Patel, observing a spotted hyena that has arrived on his lifeboat after a shipwreck.

## Extract Summary

The extract begins with Pi's general observations about hyenas (lines 1–6), then moves into a detailed, pejorative description of the spotted hyena's appearance (lines 10–19), and concludes with the hyena's wild, erratic behaviour (line 20 to the end). The tone shifts from detached observation to vivid, almost comic disgust, and finally to a tense, ambiguous threat.

## Key Language Features

- **Pejorative adjectives**: 'ugly beyond redemption', 'shaggy, coarse', 'bungled mix of colours', 'scraggly and unwagging'
- **Similes**: 'like the symptoms of a skin disease', 'like that of a bear', 'like no dog anyone would want as a pet'
- **Listing**: Accumulation of negative physical details (head, ears, mouth, nostrils, tail)
- **Sentence forms**: Short declarative sentences ('The mouth is forever open and panting.') contrasted with longer, complex descriptive sentences

## Key Structural Features

- **Focus shift**: From general hyena facts to specific physical description to behavioural observation
- **Zoom-in technique**: Moving from broad statements to close-up physical details
- **Tonal shift**: From neutral observation to disgust to tense uncertainty

## WLV Relevance

This extract is excellent practice for **Q1 (explicit retrieval)**, **Q2 (language analysis)**, and **Q3 (structural analysis)** for the WLV English Equivalency test. The rich descriptive language and clear structural shifts make it ideal for analytical practice.
