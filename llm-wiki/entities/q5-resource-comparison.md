# Q5 Resource Comparison

This page compares the Q5 (creative writing) resources identified in the [[search-lan-research-log]] across eight sources. The comparison is designed to help WLV candidates select the most useful resources for their preparation.

## Sources Compared

| Source | Resource Type | Content Focus | WLV Relevance |
|--------|---------------|---------------|---------------|
| Save My Exams | Model answers + revision notes | Narrative and descriptive writing, planning, structure | High — structured exemplars |
| PMT Education | Guide | Q5 strategy and technique | High — focused guidance |
| MME Revise | Model solutions + revision page | Full paper model solutions, Q5 revision | High — paper-specific exemplars |

## Key Differences

### Model Answers vs Guides
- **Model answers** (Save My Exams, MME Revise, GCSE Ninja, Scrbbly) provide concrete exemplars showing what a Grade 9 response looks like. These are useful for benchmarking and deconstructing technique.
- **Guides** (PMT, English Made Simple, Redbridge Tuition, Remarkable AI) provide strategic advice on how to approach the question. These are useful for understanding the marking criteria and planning.

### Narrative vs Descriptive Focus
- **Save My Exams** provides separate model answers for narrative and descriptive writing, allowing targeted practice.
- **Other sources** tend to blend both modes or focus on one. PMT and Redbridge Tuition cover both but without separate exemplars.

### Quality and Authority
- **Save My Exams** and **MME Revise** are professionally produced and generally aligned with AQA mark schemes.
- **GCSE Ninja** and **English Made Simple** are tutoring platforms with moderate quality control.
- **Scrbbly Blog** is a single blog post — useful but limited.
- **Remarkable AI** is AI-generated; quality and accuracy are unverified.

## Recommendations for WLV Candidates

1. **Prioritise Save My Exams** for the most comprehensive and structured Q5 resources.
2. **Use MME Revise** for paper-specific model solutions (November 2020 and 2021).
3. **Consult PMT Education** for strategic guidance on Q5 technique.
4. **Use GCSE Ninja and English Made Simple** as supplementary sources for additional examples.
5. **Avoid relying on Scrbbly Blog or Remarkable AI** as primary resources due to limited scope and unverified quality.

## WLV Adaptation Notes

All resources assume the full AQA Paper 1 format (1 hour 45 minutes, writing choice). For the WLV Equivalency test:
- **Timing:** AQA recommends 45 minutes for Q5; WLV candidates should adjust based on the 2-hour total format.
- **No choice:** WLV uses a fixed thematic prompt; practice with all prompts, not just preferred ones.
- **SPaG weighting:** WLV allocates 50% of writing marks to technical accuracy — prioritise resources that emphasise SPaG.
