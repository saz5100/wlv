# Dick Turner

Dick Turner is a character in Doris Lessing's 1950 novel *The Grass is Singing*. He is the husband of the protagonist, Mary Turner. In the AQA June 2024 Paper 1 extract, his anger towards Mary is the focus of Question 4 (evaluation).

## Character Profile

Dick is presented as an angry and domineering husband. The Q4 statement in the exam paper characterises his anger as 'really unfair' and positions Mary as 'totally powerless' in the relationship. The extract from line 18 onwards depicts the dynamics of their marriage, with Dick's anger directed at Mary.

## Thematic Significance

Dick represents the oppressive patriarchal forces within the marriage. His anger contributes to Mary's psychological distress and reinforces the novel's themes of isolation, powerlessness, and marital conflict.
