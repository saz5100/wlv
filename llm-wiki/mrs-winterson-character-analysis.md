# Mrs Winterson — Character Analysis

Mrs Winterson is Jeanette Winterson's adoptive mother in the memoir *Why Be Happy When You Could Be Normal?* She is depicted as a controlling, disapproving, and emotionally distant figure whose relationship with her daughter is marked by tension and conflict.

## Key Characteristics

### Controlling
Mrs Winterson attempts to control Jeanette's life choices, including her career. When Jeanette publishes her first novel, Mrs Winterson demands that she 'did not publish', demonstrating her desire to dictate her daughter's path.

### Disapproving
She disapproves of writing as a career, viewing it as an unsuitable or improper choice. Her disapproval is communicated through direct demands and through her refusal to engage with Jeanette's work.

### Emotionally Distant
The extract suggests a lack of emotional warmth or support. Mrs Winterson does not celebrate her daughter's achievements but instead reacts with hostility or indifference.

### Larger Than Life
Jeanette describes her mother as 'out of scale, larger than life', suggesting an overwhelming presence. The description 'She loomed up. She expanded.' conveys a sense of intimidation and dominance.

### Paradoxically Small
The line 'Only later, much later, too late, did I understand how small she was to herself' reveals a deeper complexity. Mrs Winterson's controlling behaviour may stem from her own insecurities and limitations.

## Relationship with Jeanette

The relationship is characterised by:
- **Conflict**: Open disagreement about Jeanette's career
- **Lack of communication**: Mrs Winterson refuses to have a telephone, making contact difficult
- **Power imbalance**: Mrs Winterson attempts to assert authority over her adult daughter
- **Underlying sadness**: The final revelation about Mrs Winterson's 'smallness' suggests a tragic dimension to her character
