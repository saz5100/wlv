# Worcester English Equivalency Overview

The University of Worcester English Language GCSE Equivalency test is a 2-hour assessment that differs significantly from both the AQA Paper 1 and the University of Wolverhampton (WLV) Equivalency test. It uses an oral interview model for the reading section and requires handwritten submission for the writing section.

## Test Structure

- **Total time**: 2 hours (approximately 1 hour per section)
- **Total marks**: 50 (25 for reading, 25 for writing)
- **Delivery**: Online via Collaborate, with staggered release of questions

## Section A: Reading (25 marks)

Section A is based on a single unseen prose extract. Questions 1 and 2 are sent to candidates before the interview. Questions 3–6 are delivered orally during the Collaborate interview. The section assesses comprehension, interpretation, and inference.

**Question types:**
- Q1: Multiple-choice retrieval (4 marks) — select 4 true statements from 8 options
- Q2: Inference from figurative language (2 marks) — explain the meaning of a simile or metaphor
- Q3: Tone identification (2 marks) — choose from three options (A, B, or C)
- Q4: Character interpretation (3 marks) — explain what a description reveals about a character
- Q5: Summarise relationship in own words with evidence (5 marks)
- Q6: Summarise views in own words with evidence (5 marks)

**Key differences from AQA/WLV:**
- No structural analysis question (unlike AQA Q3 and WLV)
- No language analysis question (unlike AQA Q2 and WLV)
- Questions delivered orally rather than in writing
- Emphasis on summarisation rather than analytical depth

## Section B: Writing (25 marks)

Section B must be handwritten and scanned or photographed for submission. Candidates choose one of three prompts:

- **7a**: A Career — explain career journey, decisions, and aspirations
- **7b**: My First Day — a true or fictional story
- **7c**: Career Education — persuasive argument for changes to career support

**Key differences from AQA/WLV:**
- Handwriting required (not typed)
- Career-focused prompts (unlike AQA's descriptive/narrative focus)
- Persuasive writing option (7c) not covered in AQA Q5

## Preparation Advice

- Practise oral responses to reading questions
- Develop summarisation skills (paraphrasing with evidence)
- Practise tone identification from multiple-choice options
- Prepare for career-focused writing prompts
- Practise handwriting under timed conditions
- Ensure you have scanning/photographing capability for submission
