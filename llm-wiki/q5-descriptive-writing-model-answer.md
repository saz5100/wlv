# Q5 Descriptive Writing -- Grade 9 Model Answer

## Overview

Descriptive writing requires you to describe a scene, often using a picture prompt. You do NOT need a plot or characters -- you are painting a picture with words.

## Grade 9 Model Answer (Descriptive)

**Prompt:** Describe a market scene as suggested by this picture.

> The market was a symphony of chaos. Stalls burst with colour -- pyramids of oranges glowing like captured sunlight, heaps of deep purple aubergines gleaming under the faded awnings, bunches of coriander releasing their sharp, green scent into the thick morning air. Everywhere, the vendors' cries rose and fell in a rhythm as old as the city itself.
>
> An old woman ran her gnarled fingers over a bolt of silk, her face impassive as the stallholder quoted a price four times what he expected to receive. This was negotiation as theatre, a dance as formal as a waltz. She raised an eyebrow. He shrugged. She turned to walk away -- he called her back. The silk changed hands. The dance was over.
>
> Beneath the noise lay a deeper hum: the buzz of a thousand conversations, the sizzle of oil in a wok, the clatter of crates being stacked, the shuffle of feet on worn cobblestones. The air was thick -- a heady cocktail of spices, sweat, incense, and the sweet rot of discarded fruit.
>
> And then, a lull. For a single breath, the noise dropped, the crowd paused, and the market became still. In that silence, you could hear the wind stirring the awnings, the distant cry of a gull. Then the chaos resumed, as if it had never stopped.

## Why This is Grade 9

* Sensory layering -- sight, smell, sound, touch all used
* Zoom/pan technique -- wide shot to close-up to background
* Specific details -- coriander, aubergines, wok, cobblestones
* Metaphor and simile -- "symphony of chaos", "negotiation as theatre"
* Pace variation -- long flowing paragraph to short reflective
* Cyclical structure -- starts with chaos, pauses, chaos resumes
* Sophisticated vocabulary -- impassive, gnarled, heady, lull
* Sentence variety -- simple, compound, complex, fragments
