# WLV English Equivalency Specification

## Paper 1: Reading and Comprehension

### Section A: Reading for Meaning
- 1.1 Understanding explicit information — locating facts and details
- 1.2 Understanding implicit information — inference and deduction
- 1.3 Identifying purpose and audience
- 1.4 Summarising and paraphrasing

### Section B: Language Analysis
- 2.1 Identifying word classes and grammatical features
- 2.2 Analysing language choices — vocabulary, imagery, figurative language
- 2.3 Explaining how language creates meaning

### Section C: Comparison
- 3.1 Comparing texts — similarities and differences
- 3.2 Comparing language use
- 3.3 Comparing tone and purpose

## Paper 2: Writing

### Section A: Grammar and Mechanics
- 4.1 Sentence structure — simple, compound, complex
- 4.2 Punctuation — full stops, commas, apostrophes, speech marks
- 4.3 Spelling — common patterns, homophones, prefixes, suffixes
- 4.4 Paragraphing — topic sentences, cohesion

### Section B: Written Communication
- 5.1 Writing for different purposes — inform, explain, persuade, argue
- 5.2 Writing for different audiences — formal, informal
- 5.3 Organisation and structure — introductions, conclusions, logical flow
- 5.4 Register and tone

## Assessment
- Paper 1: Reading — 1hr 30
- Paper 2: Writing — 1hr 30
- Both papers equally weighted
