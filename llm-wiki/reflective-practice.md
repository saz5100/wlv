# Reflective Practice

## Learning Through Reflection

### Reflective Models
- **Gibbs' Reflective Cycle**: Description, feelings, evaluation, analysis, conclusion, and action plan
- **Kolb's Experiential Learning Cycle**: Concrete experience, reflective observation, abstract conceptualisation, and active experimentation
- **Schön's Reflection-in-Action and Reflection-on-Action**: Thinking while doing and thinking back on what was done
- **Driscoll's What? So What? Now What?**: A simple, structured approach to reflection
- **Brookfield's Critical Lenses**: Viewing experiences through autobiographical, student, peer, and theoretical perspectives

### Applying Reflection
- **Reflective journals**: Keeping regular written reflections on learning experiences
- **Portfolio development**: Compiling evidence of learning, growth, and achievement
- **Learning logs**: Recording observations, insights, and questions from classes and activities
- **Peer discussion**: Sharing and discussing reflections with classmates and colleagues
- **Action planning**: Using reflection to identify areas for improvement and set goals

### Assessment
- **Reflective essays**: Writing critically about personal learning and professional development
- **Self-assessment**: Evaluating your own work against learning outcomes and criteria
- **Peer assessment**: Giving and receiving constructive feedback on reflective work
- **Portfolio assessment**: Demonstrating learning through curated evidence and commentary
- **Personal development planning**: Using reflection to guide ongoing professional growth
