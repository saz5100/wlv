# Academic Writing

## Writing at University Level

### Essay Writing
- **Understanding the question**: Analysing essay prompts, identifying key terms, and interpreting assessment criteria
- **Planning and structuring**: Creating outlines, organising arguments, and developing logical flow
- **Introduction writing**: Establishing context, stating your thesis, and outlining your approach
- **Body paragraphs**: Developing topic sentences, presenting evidence, and providing critical analysis
- **Conclusion writing**: Summarising key arguments, reflecting on implications, and suggesting further directions

### Referencing
- **Citation styles**: Understanding Harvard, APA, MLA, and discipline-specific referencing conventions
- **In-text citations**: Integrating sources smoothly into your writing
- **Reference lists**: Formatting bibliographies correctly and consistently
- **Reference management**: Using tools like EndNote, Zotero, or Mendeley
- **Avoiding plagiarism**: Understanding academic integrity and proper source attribution

### Critical Analysis
- **Evaluating evidence**: Assessing the strength, relevance, and reliability of sources
- **Comparing perspectives**: Synthesising different viewpoints and identifying areas of agreement and disagreement
- **Developing your voice**: Moving from description to analysis and original argument
- **Theoretical application**: Using theories and frameworks to analyse evidence
- **Limitations and gaps**: Identifying weaknesses in arguments and areas for further research

### Editing
- **Self-editing**: Reviewing your own work for clarity, coherence, and consistency
- **Proofreading**: Checking for spelling, grammar, punctuation, and formatting errors
- **Peer review**: Giving and receiving constructive feedback on written work
- **Structural editing**: Ensuring logical organisation and flow of ideas
- **Style refinement**: Improving sentence variety, word choice, and academic tone
