# AQA Paper 1 June 2024 Question Paper (The Grass is Singing)

## Overview

This is the official AQA GCSE English Language Paper 1 (8700/1) question paper for the June 2024 examination, sat on Thursday 23 May 2024 (morning session). The paper uses an extract from Doris Lessing's 1950 novel *The Grass is Singing* as its source text (Source A). The extract focuses on the protagonist Mary Turner's experience of extreme heat and her strained marriage to Dick Turner.

## Paper Structure

- **Total time:** 1 hour 45 minutes
- **Total marks:** 80
- **Section A (Reading):** 40 marks — 4 questions (Q1–Q4)
- **Section B (Writing):** 40 marks — 1 question with a choice of two tasks (Q5)

## Section A: Reading (40 marks)

### Question 1 — Explicit Retrieval (4 marks)
- **Lines:** 1–6
- **Task:** List four things about Mary from the first part of the source.
- **Assessment Objective:** AO1 (identify and interpret explicit information)

### Question 2 — Language Analysis (8 marks)
- **Lines:** 7–17
- **Task:** Analyse how the writer uses language to describe the effects of the heat.
- **Prompt:** Consider words and phrases, language features and techniques, sentence forms.
- **Assessment Objective:** AO2 (explain, comment on, and analyse how writers use language)

### Question 3 — Structural Analysis (8 marks)
- **Lines:** Whole source
- **Task:** Analyse how the writer has structured the text to interest the reader.
- **Prompt:** Consider what the writer focuses attention on at the beginning, how and why focus changes, and other structural features.
- **Assessment Objective:** AO2 (explain, comment on, and analyse how writers use structure)

### Question 4 — Evaluation (20 marks)
- **Lines:** 18 to end
- **Statement:** 'Dick's anger towards Mary is really unfair. The writer makes it clear that Mary is totally powerless in this relationship.'
- **Task:** To what extent do you agree? Consider whether Dick's anger is unfair, evaluate how the writer presents the relationship, and support with references.
- **Assessment Objective:** AO4 (evaluate texts critically and support with textual references)
- **WLV Scope Note:** This question is **out of scope** for the WLV English Equivalency test.

## Section B: Writing (40 marks)

### Question 5 — Creative Writing (40 marks)
- **Task:** A magazine has asked for contributions for their creative writing section.
- **Choice 1:** Write a description of a very hot place as suggested by a picture.
- **Choice 2:** Write a story about a disagreement.
- **Marking:** 24 marks for content and organisation (AO5), 16 marks for technical accuracy (AO6).
- **WLV Scope Note:** The writing choice is **out of scope** for the WLV English Equivalency test, which uses a single mandatory prompt.

## Relevance to WLV English Equivalency

- **Directly relevant:** Q1 (retrieval), Q2 (language analysis), Q3 (structural analysis) — these skills map directly to the WLV assessment.
- **Out of scope:** Q4 (evaluation) and the Q5 writing choice structure are not part of the WLV test.
- **Source text:** The extract from *The Grass is Singing* provides excellent practice material for language and structural analysis, particularly for sensory language and focus shifts.
