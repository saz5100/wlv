# WLV English Equivalency Overview

The University of Wolverhampton (WLV) PGCE English Equivalency test is a streamlined 2-hour on-campus assessment that maps directly onto AQA GCSE English Language Paper 1 (8700) mechanics, but with several key components removed.

## Assessment Structure

The test is divided into two sections, each worth 25 marks:

### Section A: Reading Skills (25 marks)

Candidates analyse a single unseen 19th, 20th, or 21st-century literary or non-fiction prose extract. Three skills are tested:

- **Explicit Retrieval** — Skimming to isolate four distinct, literal facts quickly (equivalent to AQA Paper 1, Q1).
- **Language Analysis** — Explaining how writers use vocabulary, sensory details, and imagery (metaphors, similes, personification) to shape meaning (equivalent to AQA Paper 1, Q2).
- **Structural Analysis** — Examining text architecture transitions, including how an author shifts focus from setting to dialogue, internal thoughts, or chronological development (equivalent to AQA Paper 1, Q3).

### Section B: Writing Skills (25 marks)

Candidates produce one continuous extended writing piece of approximately 400 words from a single mandatory prompt. There is no choice between narrative and descriptive options. Marks are awarded for:

- **Technical Accuracy (SPaG)** — Worth 50% of the writing score. Key areas include comma splicing, apostrophe placement, and sentence demarcation.
- **Punctuation Variety** — Accurate use of colons, semicolons, dashes, and question marks.
- **Tone and Structure** — Use of structural devices including one-word paragraphs, rhetorical questions, and clear paragraph transitions.

## What Is Excluded

The WLV test explicitly excludes:
- The Spoken Language Endorsement
- Paper 2 text synthesis and comparison
- The evaluation essay (AQA Paper 1, Q4)
- Writing prompt choice options

## Revision Strategy

The recommended approach is to download AQA Paper 1 past papers and practise only Questions 1, 2, 3, and 5, with a strong emphasis on SPaG proofreading. Full mock papers are not necessary given the condensed format.
