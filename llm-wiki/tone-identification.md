# Tone Identification

The University of Worcester English Equivalency test includes a tone identification question (Q3) where candidates must choose from three options (A, B, or C) to describe the writer's tone in a specific passage.

## What Is Tone?

Tone refers to the writer's attitude towards the subject or audience, conveyed through word choice, sentence structure, and punctuation. It is distinct from mood (the feeling created in the reader).

## Common Tone Categories

| Tone | Description | Example Phrases |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| Matter of fact | Straightforward, unemotional, factual | 'We always walked. We had no car and no bus money.' |
| Sentimental | Emotional, nostalgic, tender | 'I remember those days with such fondness...' |
| Heartbroken | Deeply sad, grieving, despairing | 'It was the worst moment of my life.' |
| Humorous | Light-hearted, witty, ironic | 'She was like a fairy story where size is approximate.' |
| Angry | Furious, resentful, indignant | 'How dare she treat me that way?' |
| Reflective | Thoughtful, contemplative, analytical | 'Only later, much later, too late, did I understand...' |
| Sarcastic | Bitter, mocking, ironic | 'Oh, that was just perfect, wasn't it?' |

## How to Identify Tone

### Step 1: Read the Passage Carefully
- Read the specified lines (e.g., lines 4–5)
- Consider the context within the extract

### Step 2: Analyse Word Choice
- Look for emotionally charged words
- Identify any figurative language (similes, metaphors)
- Note the register (formal, informal, colloquial)

### Step 3: Consider Sentence Structure
- Short, simple sentences often convey matter-of-fact or urgent tones
- Long, complex sentences may suggest reflection or elaboration
- Exclamations indicate strong emotion

### Step 4: Evaluate the Options
- Read all three options (A, B, C)
- Eliminate clearly incorrect options
- Choose the option that best matches the overall impression

## Example

**Passage**: 'We always walked. We had no car and no bus money.'

**Options**:
A. Matter of fact
B. Sentimental
C. Heartbroken

**Analysis**: The sentences are short and declarative. There is no emotional language or nostalgia. The tone is straightforward and unemotional.

**Answer**: A. Matter of fact

## Practice Tips

- Read widely to build familiarity with different tones
- Practise identifying tone in newspaper articles, blog posts, and literary extracts
- Create flashcards with tone words and example sentences
- Discuss tone with study partners to refine your ear
