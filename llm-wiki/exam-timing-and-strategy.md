# Exam Timing and Strategy

## WLV Equivalency Test -- 2 Hours

The WLV test is a **2-hour on-campus assessment** (shorter than AQA's 1 hour 45 minutes because there is no Q4 evaluation). You must manage your time carefully.

## Recommended Timings

| Stage | Question | Marks | Time | Notes |
|-------|----------|:-----:|:----:|-------|
| Reading time | Source text | -- | 15 mins | Read carefully, annotate, highlight |
| Q1 | Retrieval | 4 | 5 mins | List 4 facts -- no full sentences |
| Q2 | Language analysis | 8 | 10 mins | 3 paragraphs, WHAT-HOW-WHY |
| Q3 | Structure analysis | 8 | 10 mins | Whole text, beginning-middle-end |
| Q5 | Creative writing | 40 | 75 mins | 5 plan + 60 write + 10 review |
| Review | Proofread | -- | 5 mins | SPaG check, read aloud in head |
| **Total** | | **60** | **120 mins** | |

## How to Use Reading Time (15 mins)

1. **First 5 mins** -- Read the source text once for overall meaning
2. **Next 5 mins** -- Annotate: underline language features, note structural shifts, bracket retrieval-worthy facts
3. **Last 5 mins** -- Re-read Q1-Q3 and plan Q5 approach

## Timing Strategy by Question

### Q1 (5 mins -- 4 marks)
* Don't write full sentences -- just list facts
* Don't infer or add your opinion
* Do scan quickly for explicit information
* Do count your points -- you need exactly 4

### Q2 (10 mins -- 8 marks)
* 3 mins -- Find 2-3 strong quotations in the extract
* 6 mins -- Write 3 paragraphs using WHAT-HOW-WHY
* 1 min -- Quick proofread

### Q3 (10 mins -- 8 marks)
* 3 mins -- Identify 2-3 structural features across whole text
* 6 mins -- Write 3 paragraphs (beginning, middle, end)
* 1 min -- Quick proofread

### Q5 (75 mins -- 40 marks) THE BIG ONE
* 5 mins -- PLAN! Brainstorm ideas, structure, vocabulary
* 55 mins -- WRITE. Aim for 2.5-3 pages.
* 10 mins -- REVIEW. Check SPaG, paragraphing, sentence variety
* 5 mins -- Final proofread -- correct any errors you spot

## Before the Exam

* Know your timings -- memorise the time per question
* Practice with a timer -- do full past papers under exam conditions
* Prepare vocabulary banks -- learn 5-10 powerful descriptive words
* Plan story frameworks -- have a flexible structure you can adapt
* Know the mark schemes -- understand what examiners reward

## During the Exam

* Wear a watch -- there is no on-screen timer
* Read the source first -- don't jump straight into questions
* Follow your timings rigidly -- if Q2 time runs out, move to Q3
* If stuck on a question -- write something, anything, and move on
* Keep Q5 for last -- it's the most marks, but you need fresh thinking

## The 5-Minute Warning

When 5 minutes remain:
* If on Q5 -- write your final sentence and proofread
* If on earlier questions -- finish your current point and move to Q5 immediately
* Never leave Q5 blank -- it's 40 marks (67% of the test)
