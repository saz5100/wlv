# AQA Paper 1 Mock Question Paper (City of the Beasts)

This document is a mock examination paper following the AQA 8700/1 specification ('Explorations in creative reading and writing'). It consists of a question paper and a source insert.

## Source Material
The source text is an extract from the 21st-century novel *City of the Beasts* (2004) by [[isabel-allende]]. It focuses on fifteen-year-old [[alex-cold]] and the emotional turmoil his family faces due to his mother's severe illness.

## Assessment Structure

### Section A: Reading (40 Marks)
- **Question 1:** Explicit retrieval of four facts about a bird in a nightmare (4 marks).
- **Question 2:** Language analysis of the effects of a storm (8 marks).
- **Question 3:** Structural analysis of how the text interests the reader (8 marks).
- **Question 4:** Critical evaluation of whether Alex is struggling to cope with his mother's illness (20 marks).

### Section B: Writing (40 Marks)
- **Question 5:** Creative writing task with a choice between:
    - A description of a stormy sea (based on a picture).
    - A narrative starting with the sentence: 'This was going to be a terrible day, one of those days when it’s best to stay in bed because everything is going to turn out bad.'
- **Marking:** 24 marks for content and organisation; 16 marks for technical accuracy (SPaG).

## WLV Equivalency Note
While this paper follows the standard AQA format, students preparing for the WLV Equivalency test should note that **Question 4** and the **choice of tasks in Question 5** are typically out of scope according to the [[wlv-english-equivalency-blueprint]].
