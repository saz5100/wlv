# Worcester Sample Paper C — Why Be Happy When You Could Be Normal?

This is a sample paper for the University of Worcester English Language GCSE Equivalency test. It is based on an extract from Jeanette Winterson's memoir *Why Be Happy When You Could Be Normal?* (2011). The paper is structured differently from both AQA Paper 1 and the WLV Equivalency test, featuring an oral interview model for Section A and a handwritten Section B.

## Key Features

- **Oral delivery model**: Section A questions 3–6 are delivered orally during a Collaborate interview, not seen in advance.
- **Staggered release**: Questions are released in three phases — Q1–2 before the interview, Q3–6 at the interview start, and the writing task 5 minutes before invigilation.
- **Handwriting requirement**: Section B must be handwritten and scanned or photographed for submission.
- **50/50 mark split**: 25 marks for reading, 25 marks for writing.
- **No structural analysis question**: Unlike AQA Q3 (8 marks) and the WLV test, this paper has no dedicated structural analysis question.
- **No language analysis question**: Unlike AQA Q2 (8 marks), there is no explicit language analysis question — Q2 asks about a simile's meaning (2 marks only).
- **Summarisation emphasis**: Q5 and Q6 require summarising in own words with evidence, testing comprehension rather than analytical depth.

## Section A: Reading (25 marks)

| Question | Skill | Marks |
|----------|-------|-------|
| Q1 | Multiple-choice retrieval (select 4 true statements from 8) | 4 |
| Q2 | Inference from figurative language (simile) | 2 |
| Q3 | Tone identification (choose A, B, or C) | 2 |
| Q4 | Interpretation of character description | 3 |
| Q5 | Summarise relationship in own words with evidence | 5 |
| Q6 | Summarise views in own words with evidence | 5 |

## Section B: Writing (25 marks)

Three options are offered:
- **7a**: A Career — explain career journey, decisions, and aspirations.
- **7b**: My First Day — a true or fictional story.
- **7c**: Career Education — persuasive argument for changes to career support.

## Assessment Criteria

- Section A: Assessed on comprehension, interpretation, and inference.
- Section B: Assessed on quality of writing, including good English and clear presentation.
