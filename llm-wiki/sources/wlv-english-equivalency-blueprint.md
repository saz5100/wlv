# WLV English Equivalency Blueprint

This document is a targeted revision map for the University of Wolverhampton (WLV) PGCE English Equivalency test. It strips the AQA GCSE English Language (8700) specification down to the 2-hour on-campus assessment format, identifying exactly which components to study and which to skip.

## Key Content

The blueprint defines two sections:

- **Section A: Reading Skills (25 marks)** — Focus on a single unseen 19th, 20th, or 21st-century literary or non-fiction prose extract. Skills required: explicit retrieval (Q1 style), language analysis (Q2 style), and structural analysis (Q3 style).
- **Section B: Writing Skills (25 marks)** — One continuous extended writing piece (approx. 400 words) from a single mandatory prompt. Emphasis on technical accuracy (SPaG, worth 50% of writing marks), punctuation variety, and tone/structure.

## Components to Skip

The blueprint explicitly excludes:
- The Spoken Language Endorsement
- Paper 2 text synthesis and comparison
- The evaluation essay (AQA Paper 1, Q4)
- Writing prompt choice options (single fixed prompt only)

## Revision Strategy

The document recommends downloading AQA Paper 1 past papers and practising only Questions 1, 2, 3, and 5, with a strong focus on SPaG proofreading.
