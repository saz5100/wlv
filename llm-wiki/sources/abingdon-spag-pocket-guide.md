# Abingdon SPaG Pocket Guide

The **Abingdon SPaG Pocket Guide** is a comprehensive technical resource produced by the [[abingdon-english-department]]. It provides a foundational framework for English grammar, punctuation, and spelling, designed to support students in achieving technical accuracy in their writing.

## Content Overview
The guide is structured into four primary domains:
1. **Grammar**: Classification of word classes (parts of speech), sentence definitions, clause types, and sentence classifications.
2. **Punctuation**: Detailed rules for capitalisation, commas, apostrophes, semicolons, colons, and other punctuation marks (brackets, dashes, ellipsis, exclamation marks).
3. **Writing Structure**: Guidelines for paragraphing in both analytical and creative contexts.
4. **Spelling**: Strategies for mastering difficult words, a list of 100 common misspellings, homophone distinctions, and irregular plurals.

## Application to WLV Equivalency
This source provides the technical definitions and rules that underpin the [[spag-reference]] and [[writing-guide]] pages. Given that SPaG accounts for 50% of the writing marks in the WLV assessment, the rules regarding [[comma-splice]], [[semicolon-usage]], and [[colon-usage]] are critical for candidates.
