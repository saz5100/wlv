# AQA Paper 1 June 2022 Question Paper

This document is the official question paper for the AQA GCSE English Language Paper 1 (Explorations in Creative Reading and Writing) from June 2022. It provides the specific prompts and mark allocations used to assess students' abilities in reading and writing.

## Paper Structure

The paper is divided into two main sections, each worth 40 marks, for a total of 80 marks.

### Section A: Reading (40 Marks)
Based on **Source A** (an extract from *The Pearl* by John Steinbeck), this section contains four questions:
- **Question 1**: Explicit retrieval of four facts from lines 1–6 (4 marks).
- **Question 2**: Language analysis focusing on the conflict between Kino and the scorpion in lines 7–17 (8 marks).
- **Question 3**: Structural analysis of the whole source, focusing on how the writer interests the reader (8 marks).
- **Question 4**: Critical evaluation of the characters' reactions to danger, specifically comparing Juana and Kino from line 19 to the end (20 marks).

### Section B: Writing (40 Marks)
A creative writing task (Question 5) with a choice of two prompts:
- **Option 1**: A description of an unusual place to stay (based on a provided image).
- **Option 2**: A story about a life-saving rescue.

**Marking Breakdown for Q5:**
- Content and Organisation: 24 marks.
- Technical Accuracy (SPaG): 16 marks.

## WLV Equivalency Context
While this paper serves as a primary practice resource, there are key differences between this standard AQA paper and the WLV assessment:
- **Scope**: Question 4 (Critical Evaluation) is **out of scope** for the WLV test.
- **Writing Choice**: The WLV test uses a **fixed thematic prompt** rather than a choice between two options.
- **Timing**: The AQA paper allows 1 hour 45 minutes, whereas the WLV assessment is 2 hours.
