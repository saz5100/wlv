# OCR Little Book of SPaG

The *Little Book of Spelling, Punctuation and Grammar (SPaG)*, authored by Frank Danes (OCR, 2020), serves as a targeted reference guide for identifying and correcting common linguistic pitfalls. It is specifically designed to address the technical errors that most frequently frustrate GCSE examiners, making it a critical resource for the writing component (Q5) of the WLV English Equivalency assessment, where technical accuracy carries a 50% weighting.

## Core Philosophy
The guide posits that English is a fluid language and that "correctness" is often a reflection of currently acceptable usage. However, it maintains a strict standard for formal academic writing to ensure clarity and professionalism.

## Key Areas of Focus
- **Punctuation as Rhythm**: The guide uses musical metaphors (Crotchet, Minim, Semi-breve) to explain how different punctuation marks create pauses of varying lengths.
- **Common Pitfalls**: Detailed guidance on "the two most irritating mistakes": comma splicing and apostrophe misuse.
- **Precision and Formalism**: Distinctions between similar-sounding words (homophones) and the preference for academic terms (e.g., 'quotation' over 'quote').
- **UK vs US English**: Identification of Americanisms and the enforcement of British English standards (e.g., '-ise' endings and the avoidance of split infinitives).
