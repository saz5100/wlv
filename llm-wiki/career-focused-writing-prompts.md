# Career-Focused Writing Prompts

The University of Worcester English Equivalency test includes career-focused writing prompts (Q7a and Q7c) that differ from the descriptive/narrative focus of AQA Paper 1 and the WLV test. These prompts require candidates to write about their own career journey or to argue for changes to career education.

## Prompt Types

### 7a: A Career (Explanatory/Reflective)
Explain where you are in terms of your career and how you arrived at this point. Explain the decisions you have made and how and why you made them. Talk also about your hopes and aspirations for the future.

**Key skills**: Reflection, explanation, personal narrative, future planning

### 7c: Career Education (Persuasive)
Choose one or two changes which you would suggest to improve support for young people and/or adults choosing a career or seeking to change career. Explain the changes you would recommend, and persuade the reader of their value.

**Key skills**: Persuasion, argumentation, evidence, structure

## Structure for 7a (Explanatory/Reflective)

### Introduction
- Briefly state your current career position
- Outline the scope of your reflection

### Body Paragraph 1: Past Decisions
- Describe key decisions that led to your current position
- Explain the reasoning behind each decision
- Mention any challenges or turning points

### Body Paragraph 2: Present Situation
- Describe where you are now
- Reflect on how past decisions shaped the present
- Acknowledge any ongoing challenges

### Body Paragraph 3: Future Aspirations
- Outline your hopes and goals
- Explain steps you plan to take
- Connect future plans to past experiences

### Conclusion
- Summarise your journey
- Reflect on lessons learned
- End with a forward-looking statement

## Structure for 7c (Persuasive)

### Introduction
- State your position clearly
- Identify the two changes you will recommend
- Engage the reader's interest

### Body Paragraph 1: First Change
- Describe the change in detail
- Explain why it is needed (problem)
- Persuade the reader of its value (solution)
- Use evidence or examples

### Body Paragraph 2: Second Change
- Describe the second change
- Explain its necessity and benefits
- Connect to the first change for coherence

### Conclusion
- Summarise your recommendations
- Reinforce the urgency or importance
- End with a call to action

## Language Features for Persuasive Writing

- **Rhetorical questions**: 'How can we expect young people to make informed choices without proper guidance?'
- **Emotive language**: 'Too many talented individuals are being let down by a system that fails to support them.'
- **Statistics and evidence**: 'Research shows that 60% of adults feel unprepared for their career choices.'
- **Triadic structure**: 'We need better advice, more experience, and greater support.'
- **Imperatives**: 'We must act now to change this.'

## Common Pitfalls

- **Being too vague**: Use specific examples and evidence
- **Losing focus**: Stick to one or two changes for 7c
- **Forgetting persuasion**: 7c requires persuasive language, not just explanation
- **Ignoring structure**: Plan your response before writing
- **Poor SPaG**: Career-focused writing still requires good English and clear presentation
