# Critical Thinking

## Developing Critical Thinking Skills

### Analytical Frameworks
- **Bloom's Taxonomy**: Using levels of thinking from recall to evaluation and creation
- **SWOT analysis**: Assessing strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats in arguments and situations
- **Critical thinking models**: Applying frameworks such as Paul and Elder's Elements of Thought
- **Logical reasoning**: Understanding deductive and inductive reasoning, and identifying logical fallacies
- **Evidence evaluation**: Assessing the quality, relevance, and sufficiency of evidence

### Argument Analysis
- **Identifying arguments**: Recognising claims, premises, and conclusions in texts and discussions
- **Evaluating assumptions**: Surfacing and questioning underlying assumptions in arguments
- **Assessing reasoning**: Determining whether conclusions follow logically from premises
- **Detecting bias**: Identifying personal, cultural, and systemic biases in arguments
- **Counter-argumentation**: Anticipating and responding to opposing viewpoints

### Problem-Solving
- **Problem identification**: Defining problems clearly and breaking them into manageable components
- **Creative thinking**: Generating innovative solutions through brainstorming and lateral thinking
- **Decision-making**: Evaluating options using criteria, evidence, and risk assessment
- **Reflective practice**: Learning from problem-solving experiences and outcomes
- **Collaborative problem-solving**: Working with others to generate and evaluate solutions
