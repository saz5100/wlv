# Academic Skills

## Core Academic Skills for Higher Education

### Critical Reading
- **Active reading techniques**: Previewing, annotating, questioning, and summarising texts to engage deeply with material
- **Evaluating sources**: Assessing credibility, relevance, bias, and currency of academic sources
- **Identifying arguments**: Recognising main claims, supporting evidence, and logical structure in academic writing
- **Note-taking strategies**: Cornell method, mind mapping, and digital tools for effective note organisation
- **Reading for research**: Skimming for key information, scanning for specific details, and close reading for analysis

### Academic Writing
- **Essay structure**: Developing clear introductions, coherent body paragraphs, and strong conclusions
- **Academic style**: Using formal language, appropriate tone, and discipline-specific conventions
- **Paragraph development**: Topic sentences, evidence, analysis, and linking ideas
- **Argumentation**: Building logical arguments with claims, evidence, and reasoning
- **Referencing**: Understanding citation styles (Harvard, APA, MLA) and avoiding plagiarism

### Research Skills
- **Information literacy**: Locating, evaluating, and using information effectively
- **Library skills**: Using academic databases, catalogues, and subject-specific resources
- **Search strategies**: Developing effective keywords, Boolean operators, and search filters
- **Data management**: Organising, storing, and documenting research data
- **Ethical research**: Understanding consent, confidentiality, and academic integrity

### Time Management
- **Planning and prioritisation**: Using calendars, to-do lists, and project management tools
- **Avoiding procrastination**: Breaking tasks into manageable chunks and setting realistic deadlines
- **Study techniques**: Pomodoro method, spaced repetition, and active recall
- **Work-life balance**: Managing study alongside work, family, and personal commitments
- **Goal setting**: SMART goals and regular progress reviews
