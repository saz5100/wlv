# Life of Pi June 2023 Extract Analysis

This guide provides a breakdown of the "What, How, and Why" for the June 2023 extract, focusing on the interaction between Pi and the spotted hyena.

## Language Analysis (Q2 Focus)
**What:** The writer creates a sense of repulsion and unpredictable threat.
**How:** 
- **Grotesque Imagery:** The hyena is "ugly beyond redemption" with a coat like "symptoms of a skin disease." This suggests something diseased and unnatural.
- **Onomatopoeia:** The "yip yip yip" and "clicking" of claws create an auditory landscape of agitation.
- **Comparative Language:** Comparing the hyena to a "bear" (massive head) but also "mouse-like" (ears) emphasises its distorted, unnatural appearance.
**Why:** To make the reader share Pi's visceral disgust and fear, establishing the hyena as a symbol of chaos and danger.

## Structural Analysis (Q3 Focus)
**What:** The narrative moves from intellectual fear to physical terror, ending in a sudden release.
**How:**
- **Opening:** Establishes a foundation of knowledge (Father's words) which frames the hyena as a "hardy attacker."
- **The Build-up:** The use of the "indoor track" metaphor and the counting of laps ("one lap-two-three...") creates a rhythmic escalation of tension.
- **The Pivot:** The moment the hyena stops and looks at Pi ("I prepared for my end") is the peak of the tension.
- **The Resolution:** The [[anticlimax]] of the hyena vomiting breaks the tension and shifts the focus to the animal's vulnerability.
**Why:** To mirror the psychological experience of a survivor—the agonizing wait for a blow that may or may not come.

## Key Evidence for High Marks
- **"Maniacal beast"**: Suggests madness and lack of control.
- **"Blank and frank"**: Describes the hyena's gaze, suggesting a lack of empathy or reason.
- **"Furthest confines of animal anguish"**: High-level vocabulary describing the hyena's state after vomiting.
