# Q5 Creative Writing Strategy

## Overview

Question 5 is the **writing task** -- worth **40 marks** (50% of Paper 1 marks). You choose between **descriptive writing** (picture prompt) or **narrative writing** (story opening/title). The WLV test uses a **fixed thematic prompt** (no choice -- one prompt only).

## Time Management for Q5

| Stage | Time | What to do |
|-------|------|-----------|
| Plan | 5 mins | Brainstorm ideas, structure, vocabulary |
| Write | 35 mins | Aim for 2.5-3 pages |
| Review | 5 mins | Check SPaG, paragraphing, sentence variety |

## Structure for Descriptive Writing

1. **Opening paragraph** -- Establish the scene using sensory details (sight, sound, smell, touch). Zoom in on one key image.
2. **Middle paragraphs (2-3)** -- Explore different aspects of the scene: shift focus from foreground to background, from one sense to another, from stillness to movement.
3. **Closing paragraph** -- Return to the overall atmosphere. End with a striking image or reflective thought.

## Structure for Narrative Writing

1. **Opening** -- Drop the reader straight into action or intrigue. Establish character and setting quickly.
2. **Build-up** -- Introduce conflict or tension. Use short sentences for pace, longer ones for description.
3. **Climax** -- The most intense moment. Switch to shorter paragraphs and sensory language.
4. **Resolution** -- Show the aftermath. Leave the reader with a lingering image or feeling.

## Grade 9 Model Answer (Narrative)

**Prompt:** Write a story about a life-saving rescue.

> The sea was a living thing that night -- black, heaving, hungry. Liam stood at the water's edge, the spray stinging his face like a thousand needles. Somewhere out there, beyond the roar of the waves, a child was screaming.
>
> He didn't think. He ran.
>
> The cold hit him like a wall, stealing his breath. Each stroke was a battle against the current, his muscles screaming in protest. The lighthouse beam swept across the water, and for one split second, he saw her -- a small figure clinging to a capsized boat.
>
> "Hold on!" he roared, the wind ripping the words from his mouth.
>
> She was barely conscious when he reached her, lips blue, eyes half-closed. He grabbed her jacket, wrapped an arm around her chest, and began the desperate swim back. The shore seemed impossibly far. His lungs burned. His legs were lead.
>
> Then -- hands. Strong hands pulling them both from the frothing water. The beach. Safety.
>
> Liam collapsed onto the sand, gasping, as the girl coughed up seawater and began to cry. He closed his eyes, letting the rain wash over him. He had never felt so alive.

## What Makes This Grade 9

* Sensory immersion -- spray stinging, cold like a wall, lungs burning
* Short sentences for pace -- "He didn't think. He ran."
* Sentence fragments for impact -- "The beach. Safety."
* Varied sentence openings -- atmospheric then action-driven
* Powerful verbs -- heaving, roared, ripped, frothing
* Sophisticated punctuation -- dashes, sentence fragments
* Emotional arc -- from danger through desperation to relief

## Common Q5 Mistakes to Avoid

* No plan leads to wandering plot or unfinished story
* Writing below 2 pages means insufficient content for top marks
* Forgetting paragraphs -- capped at Level 2 for AO5
* Repetitive vocabulary -- use a thesaurus in your head
* Telling instead of showing -- "He was scared" becomes "His heart hammered against his ribs"
* Ignoring SPaG -- AO6 is worth 16 marks

## Key Techniques for Top Marks

* Show, don't tell -- use sensory details, actions, and dialogue
* Zoom and pan -- alternate between wide shots and close-ups
* Pace variation -- short sentences for tension, long sentences for atmosphere
* Powerful vocabulary -- precise nouns and vivid verbs
* Punctuation range -- colons, semicolons, dashes
* Sentence variety -- simple, compound, complex, fragments
