The Spoken Language Endorsement
Standard school GCSE candidates must present
a graded, filmed speech or presentation to an
audience. Completely ignore this component.

Paper 1 vs Paper 2 Text Synthesis
The full GCSE forces you to compare, cross-
reference, and synthesize viewpoints across two
entirely distinct historical texts (Paper 2, Q4).
WLV uses a single source extract; avoid
synthesis drill exercises.

The Evaluation Essay Question
AQA Paper 1, Question 4 demands a lengthy 20-
mark evaluation debating a specific critical
student statement. This heavy essay format is
stripped out of the 2-hour format.

Writing Prompts Choice Options
The full AQA examination gives candidates an
"either/or" creative choice (e.g., choosing
between writing a narrative story or a descriptive
photo prompt). The university assessment
generally delivers a single, fixed thematic
prompt.

Section A: Reading Skills (25 Marks)
Focus entirely on unpicking a single unseen
19th, 20th, or 21st-century literary or non-fiction
prose extract.

•

•

•

Explicit Retrieval: Practice skimming to isolate
4 distinct, literal facts quickly (AQA Paper 1, Q1
style).

Language Analysis: Learn to explain how
writers use vocabulary, sensory details, and
imagery (metaphors, similes, personification) to
shape meaning.

Structural Analysis: Master text architecture
transitions—how an author shifts focus from
setting to dialogue, internal thoughts, or
chronological development.

✓

Section B: Writing Skills (25 Marks)
You must construct one continuous extended
writing piece (approx. 400 words) from a single
mandatory prompt.

•

•

•

Technical Accuracy (SPaG): Worth 50% of
your writing score. Revise comma splicing,
apostrophe placement, and sentence
demarcation.

Punctuation Variety: Force yourself to
accurately use advanced marks including
colons, semicolons, dashes, and question
marks.

Tone & Structure: Practice structural devices
including one-word paragraphs, rhetorical
questions, and clear paragraph transitions to
build a persuasive or descriptive arc.

Streamlined Revision Execution Plan

Because the test maps perfectly to AQA mechanics but condenses the layout, do not waste energy on full mock
papers. Use your revision time strictly practicing the following core components:

1. Download AQA Paper 1 Past Papers

2. Practice Questions 1, 2 & 3 (Reading)

3. Practice Question 5 (Writing)

4. Proofread for SPaG
