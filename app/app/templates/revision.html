📘 WLV — Core Study Areas

Key revision topics for University of Wolverhampton preparation programmes

1 Academic Skills

📚 Core Academic Skills

  • Critical reading: Previewing, annotating, questioning, and summarising texts. Evaluate sources for credibility, relevance, bias, and currency.
  • Note-taking strategies: Cornell method, mind mapping, outline method, and digital tools for effective organisation.
  • Essay planning: Brainstorming, thesis development, outlining arguments, and structuring paragraphs.
  • Time management: Prioritisation matrices, study schedules, Pomodoro technique, and avoiding procrastination.

📝 Academic Writing

  • Essay structure: Introduction (thesis + roadmap), body paragraphs (PEEL/TEEL), conclusion (synthesis + implications).
  • Academic tone: Formal language, third person, evidence-based claims, cautious language (hedging).
  • Referencing: Harvard style — in-text citations (Author, Year) and reference list. Secondary referencing and paraphrasing.
💡 Tip: University essays are judged on argument and evidence, not just knowledge. Always ask: "So what? Why does this matter?"
2 Critical Thinking

🧠 What is Critical Thinking?

  • Analysis: Breaking down arguments into claims, evidence, and assumptions
  • Evaluation: Assessing the strength of evidence and reasoning
  • Inference: Drawing logical conclusions from available evidence
  • Explanation: Articulating reasoning clearly and coherently
  • Self-regulation: Reflecting on your own thinking processes and biases

🔍 Analysing Arguments

  • Premise: A statement offered as evidence or reason
  • Conclusion: The main claim the argument supports
  • Assumptions: Unstated beliefs that connect premises to conclusion
  • Fallacies: Common errors in reasoning (straw man, ad hominem, false dichotomy, slippery slope)

📊 Evaluation Frameworks

  • CRAAP test: Currency, Relevance, Authority, Accuracy, Purpose — for evaluating sources
  • PROMPT: Presentation, Relevance, Objectivity, Method, Provenance, Timeliness — another source evaluation framework
  • SWOT analysis: Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats — for evaluating arguments and positions

📋 Data Collection Methods

  • Surveys and questionnaires: Closed and open questions, Likert scales, sampling strategies
  • Interviews: Structured, semi-structured, unstructured — each has different strengths
  • Focus groups: Group discussion for exploring attitudes and perceptions
  • Observations: Participant vs non-participant, structured vs unstructured
  • Document analysis: Policy documents, reports, media, historical sources

⚖️ Research Ethics

  • Informed consent: Participants must understand what they are agreeing to
  • Confidentiality: Protecting participants' identities and data
  • Right to withdraw: Participants can leave the study at any time
  • Avoiding harm: Physical, psychological, and emotional wellbeing of participants
  • Ethical approval: Most research requires approval from an ethics committee
4 Professional Practice

💼 Workplace Skills

  • Communication: Verbal, written, and digital communication in professional contexts
  • Teamwork: Collaborative working, group dynamics, conflict resolution
  • Leadership: Styles of leadership, motivating others, delegation
  • Time management: Prioritisation, meeting deadlines, work-life balance

🔄 Reflective Practice

  • Gibbs' Reflective Cycle: Description → Feelings → Evaluation → Analysis → Conclusion → Action Plan
  • Kolb's Learning Cycle: Concrete Experience → Reflective Observation → Abstract Conceptualisation → Active Experimentation
  • Schön's reflection-in-action: Thinking on your feet during professional practice
  • Reflective writing is a key skill for professional portfolios and assignments

📊 Personal Development

  • Setting SMART goals (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)
  • Creating a Personal Development Plan (PDP)
  • Seeking and responding to feedback
  • Building a professional portfolio of evidence
5 Digital Literacy

💻 Digital Skills for University

  • Online research: Using academic databases (JSTOR, Google Scholar, Scopus), advanced search techniques, evaluating online sources
  • Data analysis: Spreadsheets for data organisation, basic statistical analysis, creating charts and graphs
  • Presentation skills: Creating effective presentations, using visual aids, public speaking techniques
  • Digital collaboration: Using shared documents, project management tools, virtual meeting platforms

🔐 Digital Safety

  • Data protection and GDPR awareness
  • Cyber security basics — passwords, phishing, secure connections
  • Digital footprint management
  • Academic integrity online — avoiding plagiarism, using AI appropriately
6 Subject Knowledge

📖 Developing Subject Expertise

  • Reading lists: Use your module reading list as a starting point, then expand through database searches
  • Key concepts: Identify and understand the core concepts and theories in your discipline
  • Academic discourse: Learn the language and conventions of your subject area
  • Current research: Stay up to date with recent publications and debates in your field

📝 Assessment Preparation

  • Understand assessment criteria and learning outcomes
  • Use feedback from previous assignments to improve
  • Plan your time across multiple assessments
  • Seek support from tutors, study skills services, and peer groups
